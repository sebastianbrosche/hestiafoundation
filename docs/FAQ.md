# Hestia Foundation — Frequently Asked Questions

Last updated: 2026-05-29

## The Project

### What is Hestia Foundation?
Hestia Foundation is a Portuguese non-profit developing open-source construction systems for affordable, permanent, off-grid family housing. We publish complete build manuals, material lists, cost data, and performance data so anyone can replicate our designs.

### Where are you based?
Lagos, Algarve, Portugal. Our first prototype (Shell A) will be built in the Lagos area.

### When did you start?
May 2026. We are a new organisation with an ambitious timeline: prototype build in Q3 2026, major grant applications by end of 2026.

### Is this a company?
No. Hestia Foundation is being registered as an Associação (non-profit association) under Portuguese law. We do not sell houses, kits, or plans. Everything is free.

## The Building System

### What is the Ribbed Composite Panel (RCP)?
The RCP is our building system that uses standard EPAL Euro pallets (1200×800 mm) as the primary structural element. Pallets are arranged in interlocking grids, reinforced with I-joists, and sheathed with OSB or cement-board.

### How much does it cost to build a Shell A?
Materials only: approximately €10,000–15,000 for a 37.5 m² weathertight shell. With electrics, plumbing, insulation, and interior finishes: €15,000–20,000. With labour costs: €20,000–25,000.

### How long does it take to build?
10 active build days with a team of 4–6 people. Spread across 2–4 weeks including drying/curing time.

### What skills do I need?
No specialist construction skills required. Basic carpentry knowledge helps but is not essential. We provide a complete build manual with step-by-step photos and videos.

### Are the plans really free?
Yes. All designs, build manuals, CAD files, cost data, and performance data are published under CC BY-SA 4.0. You can download, modify, build, and even sell your modifications — as long as you share your improvements under the same license.

### Can I build this myself?
Yes. The system is designed for self-build by non-specialists. However, we recommend having one experienced builder on the team for safety and quality.

### Do I need a building permit?
In Portugal, structures under 50 m² with temporary or lightweight foundations may not require a full building permit. However, you must check with your local Câmara Municipal (municipality). We are working with Lagos Municipality to clarify requirements.

### What about structural engineering sign-off?
For the prototype, we are engaging a Portuguese-licensed structural engineer to review calculations and sign documentation. For personal builds, requirements vary by jurisdiction. We recommend consulting a local engineer.

### Is it safe?
EPAL pallets are rated for 1,500 kg dynamic load. Our system uses 80–100 pallets in a redundant grid with I-joist reinforcement and OSB sheathing. The prototype will be independently tested by LNEC (National Laboratory for Civil Engineering).

### What about fire safety?
All materials are treated for fire resistance where required. Mineral wool insulation is non-combustible. OSB sheathing can be treated with fire-retardant coatings. Local fire codes apply.

### What about pests (termites, rodents)?
The prototype uses heat-treated (HT) pallets (marked "HT" on the pallet) which are free of chemicals and less attractive to pests than raw timber. Regular inspection and maintenance are recommended.

## Performance

### Is it warm in winter and cool in summer?
Shell A uses 100–150 mm mineral wool insulation in walls, roof, and floor. This provides U-values of 0.25–0.35 W/m²K — comparable to conventional Portuguese timber frame construction. The 12-month sensor monitoring will provide empirical data.

### What is the expected lifespan?
With proper maintenance (external cladding, roof protection, moisture management), 20–30 years. The prototype monitoring will help us determine actual durability.

### Can it withstand storms and wind?
The system is designed for Algarve wind loads (Eurocode 1). The roof is secured with hurricane straps. The low profile (single storey) reduces wind exposure. Independent testing will verify.

### Is it waterproof?
Yes. The roof has a breathable waterproof membrane. Walls have external membrane and optional rainscreen cladding. The floor is raised on foundations to prevent ground moisture.

### Can I add solar panels?
Yes. The roof structure can support standard solar panel loads. We recommend installing panels during the build phase for easier access.

### Can I connect to mains water and electricity?
Yes. The build manual includes electrics and plumbing for both on-grid and off-grid configurations.

### Can I expand it later?
Yes. The modular pallet grid allows easy extension. You can add rooms by building additional wall panels and connecting them to the existing structure.

## Materials and Sourcing

### Where do I get EPAL pallets?
EPAL pallets are widely available across Europe. In Portugal, check:
- Local logistics companies and warehouses
- Pallet recyclers and reconditioners
- Online marketplaces (OLX, Facebook Marketplace)
- Direct from EPAL-licensed manufacturers

Typical cost: €8–12 per used pallet in good condition.

### What condition do the pallets need to be in?
Structurally sound: no broken stringers, no rot, no chemical contamination. Look for "EPAL" and "HT" (heat-treated) marks. Avoid pallets with spills, stains, or unusual odours.

### Can I use other types of pallets?
The build manual is designed for EPAL Euro pallets (1200×800 mm). Other sizes can be adapted but require modifications to the plans.

### What about I-joists, OSB, and insulation?
Available at any builders merchant (Leroy Merlin, AKI, etc.) in Portugal or EU. We provide a full Bill of Materials with quantities and approximate costs.

### Is this sustainable?
Yes. Pallets are a waste stream (2.5 billion circulate in EU annually). Using them for housing extends their life and reduces waste. The system avoids concrete (8% of global CO₂) and structural steel. All materials are recyclable or biodegradable at end of life.

## Volunteering and Workshops

### Can I help build the prototype?
Yes! We need 4–6 build team members for Q3 2026. See `docs/VOLUNTEER_RECRUITMENT.md` for details. No experience required — enthusiasm and willingness to learn are enough.

### Is there a cost to participate?
No. We provide meals during build days. Accommodation can be arranged at no cost (shared house, camping, or hostel). You arrange your own travel to Lagos.

### Can I do a thesis on this?
Yes. We welcome student researchers from civil engineering, architecture, environmental science, and data analysis. Potential thesis topics:
- Structural performance of pallet-based composite panels
- Thermal behaviour in Mediterranean climate
- Life cycle assessment (LCA) of pallet construction
- Social acceptance of alternative building systems

### Can my university partner with you?
Yes. We are actively seeking partnerships with University of Algarve and other institutions. See `docs/outreach/outreach_university_alg.md` for an example partnership email.

## Legal and Open Source

### What license do you use?
CC BY-SA 4.0 (Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International). You can:
- Use the designs for any purpose, including commercial builds
- Modify and adapt the designs
- Share your modifications

You must:
- Credit Hestia Foundation
- Share your modifications under the same license

### Can I sell houses built with your system?
Yes. You can build and sell pallet houses using our plans. You must credit Hestia Foundation and share any improvements you make to the system.

### Can I patent something based on your system?
No. The system is published as prior art, which prevents patenting. We believe housing knowledge should be a public good, not private property.

### Who is liable if something goes wrong?
Hestia Foundation provides open-source documentation for educational purposes. We are not a construction company and do not assume liability for individual builds. Builders are responsible for:
- Compliance with local building codes
- Engaging qualified professionals (engineers, electricians) where required
- Safe construction practices
- Structural verification for their specific site and climate
