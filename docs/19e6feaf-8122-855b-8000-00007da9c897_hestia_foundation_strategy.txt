EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Hestia Foundation is the institutional vehicle for the RCP housing system
described in this thesis. It exists to research, document, certify, and disseminate
a low-cost self-build housing methodology that directly addresses three of the EU's
most urgent policy priorities: affordable housing supply, construction sector
innovation, and low-carbon building systems.

The EU currently has more money available for affordable housing innovation than at
any point in its history. The European Commission has committed €43 billion under
the 2021-2027 budget period for housing, with €10 billion additional in 2026-2027,
and €375 billion mobilised from partner financial institutions by 2029 (European
Commission, 2026). The first-ever EU heads of state summit on housing is scheduled
for 2026.

The Hestia Foundation is positioned at the exact intersection of every programme
the EU is currently funding.

This appendix describes:
1. The EU funding landscape and where the RCP system fits
2. The Foundation structure and legal setup
3. The prototype build strategy
4. The grant application sequence
5. The partnership strategy
6. The language and framing that wins grants

PART ONE: THE EU FUNDING LANDSCAPE
================================================================================

1.1 The Housing Crisis as EU Policy Priority

The European Commission's own data confirms the scale of the problem the Hestia
Foundation addresses:

— House prices across the EU have risen by over 60 percent since 2013
— Rents have risen approximately 20 percent, with urban rents climbing higher
— Only 6 to 7 percent of EU housing stock is social housing
— 20 percent of homes remain unoccupied
— Short-term rentals surged 93 percent between 2018 and 2024
— Europe needs to build more than 2 million homes per year — approximately
  650,000 more annually than the 1.6 million currently built
— Meeting this demand requires investment of approximately €153 billion per year
  (European Commission, 2026)

The Commission's European Affordable Housing Plan explicitly calls for:
— Innovation in the construction sector
— Simplified and digitalised permitting procedures
— Accelerated development of harmonised standards for innovative construction
  products, materials and methods
— Scaling up of innovative building solutions through the New European Bauhaus

The RCP system addresses every single one of these goals simultaneously.

1.2 The Three Funding Pillars

Three EU programmes form the core funding strategy for the Hestia Foundation.
Each addresses a different phase of development.

PILLAR ONE: NEW EUROPEAN BAUHAUS (NEB)
Best fit for the Hestia Foundation at every stage.

The NEB Facility 2025-2027 has a yearly indicative budget of €120 million for its
Research and Innovation component, anchored in Horizon Europe. It funds the full
cycle from research to testing to demonstration to deployment.

The NEB's stated values are sustainability, inclusivity, and beauty. The Hestia
Home is a literal embodiment of all three simultaneously. The EU's own framing —
"beautiful, sustainable and affordable" — matches the Hestia Home's H2 heading
word for word.

Current open NEB calls (deadline 01 December 2026):

HORIZON-NEB-2026-01-REGEN-01
"Sustainable, inclusive, affordable and beautiful solutions for thermal comfort
in buildings"
Budget: €12 million total. Expected award per project: €4 million.
Direct fit: The Hestia Home's passive cooling system (Canadian well, solar chimney,
sand battery, insulated roof) is a complete answer to this call. The RCP wall's
thermal performance at 0.40 W/m²K versus conventional masonry at 1.8 W/m²K is
the core demonstration.

HORIZON-NEB-2026-01-PARTICIPATION-01
"Addressing homelessness through housing-led approaches aligned with NEB values"
Budget: €12.6 million total. Expected award: €6.3 million.
Direct fit: The RCP system is the lowest-cost route to permanent housing for
vulnerable populations. The community build model (barn-raising, grassroots
movement) aligns with the NEB's emphasis on community-led approaches.

Additional NEB calls for reuse of vacant spaces, sustainable maintenance, and
neighbourhood spatial design — all open with December 2026 deadlines.

NEB PRIZES AND BOOST 2026 (immediate opportunities):
— NEB Prizes 2026: Up to €20,000 per winning project. 13 winners total.
  Criteria: sustainability, inclusivity, quality of life.
— NEB Boost 2026: €30,000 per project. 20 winners. Must be in rural settings
  or communities under 20,000 residents.
  Criteria: circularity, carbon neutrality, affordable housing, rural regeneration.

The Algarve Lagos location qualifies for the NEB Boost. A completed prototype
with documentation, thermal performance data, and cost verification is a
competitive entry for both. These are achievable in 2026 without consortium
partners.

PILLAR TWO: HORIZON EUROPE
For the larger structural research questions.

Relevant clusters:
— Cluster 5: Climate, Energy and Mobility
— Cluster 4: Digital, Industry and Space (construction digitisation)
— Social Platform on Construction (Built4People partnership)

Key framing for Horizon Europe:
— Innovative construction systems with reduced labour dependency
— Circular building materials (upcycled industrial components)
— Climate-resilient housing at scale
— Digital fabrication and assembly protocols
— Community-led construction as social innovation

Typical Horizon Europe grants: €2 million to €8 million per project.
Consortium requirement: minimum 3 partners from different EU countries.

PILLAR THREE: LIFE PROGRAMME
For the environmental and circular economy dimensions.

The RCP system qualifies because:
— Zero concrete in the standard build (concrete = 5%+ of global CO2)
— Upcycled EPAL pallets (industrial surplus material)
— OSB from timber industry offcuts
— Natural insulation alternatives (hempcrete from Canhamor, Alentejo)
— Off-grid energy as standard
— Carbon storage in timber structure
— Passive cooling eliminating air conditioning energy demand

Each cubic metre of hempcrete used in an RCP variant sequesters approximately
160 kg of CO2. A complete hempcrete-filled RCP house is demonstrably carbon
negative.

LIFE grants typically €500,000 to €3 million. Consortium preferred but not
always required for smaller projects.

1.3 Secondary Funding Sources

INTERREG EUROPE / INTERREG NEXT PORTUGAL-SPAIN
Cross-border regional innovation. Portugal + Spain qualification.
Perfect for: Rural depopulation housing, sustainable tourism accommodation,
community housing for agricultural workers.
Typical grants: €500,000 to €2 million.

EIC ACCELERATOR
For the commercial company arm (not the foundation).
Up to €2.5 million in grant + €15 million in equity investment.
Requires: Significant innovation, scalability, market disruption potential.
The RCP system as a licensed construction methodology qualifies for disruption framing.

PORTUGAL 2030 / PRR (Plano de Recuperação e Resiliência)
Portugal's national EU recovery fund. €13.9 billion total.
Housing component: significant allocation for affordable housing innovation.
Advantage: Portuguese foundation, Portuguese project, Portuguese problem.
No consortium requirement for many instruments.

FCT (Fundação para a Ciência e a Tecnologia)
Portugal's national research foundation.
Funds: Research projects, PhD programmes, post-doctoral research.
Key opportunity: Fund the university laboratory testing of RCP panels through
an FCT research grant with a Portuguese university partner.
Typical grants: €50,000 to €500,000.

ERASMUS+ / EUROPEAN SOLIDARITY CORPS
For the educational delivery model.
The Hestia Foundation's workshop training programme — teaching communities
to build using the RCP system — qualifies as vocational education and training.
Typical grants: €50,000 to €300,000 per project.

PART TWO: THE FOUNDATION STRUCTURE
================================================================================

2.1 The Dual Structure

The recommended legal structure is a dual organisation:

HESTIA FOUNDATION (nonprofit, hestiafoundation.org)
Purpose: Research, standards development, education, pilot projects, grant
applications, open source documentation, certification pathway.

This is the entity that:
— Applies for EU grants
— Owns the research and documentation
— Publishes the open source protocol
— Partners with universities
— Runs community workshops
— Holds the CE marking application for the RCP assembly

This structure is extremely common in EU innovation ecosystems. It allows grant
funding (which requires nonprofit status for most instruments) while maintaining
the ability to license the commercial application.

HESTIA BUILD (for-profit, separate company)
Purpose: Manufacturing, commercial panel production, licensing, house sales.
This entity:
— Licenses the protocol developed by the Foundation
— Produces and sells RCP panels commercially
— Builds and sells complete Hestia Homes
— Generates revenue to partially fund the Foundation's research

2.2 Foundation Setup in Portugal

Portugal is the correct jurisdiction. Reasons:
— The housing crisis is acute and well-documented
— EU funding access is strong (Portugal 2030, PRR)
— Alentejo and Algarve are priority rural development regions
— Land is available for the prototype
— Construction costs are lower than northern Europe
— Prefabrication acceptance is growing (confirmed by Lopes, 2026)
— The Portuguese government has confirmed VAT reduction to 6 percent on
  affordable housing construction

Foundation setup in Portugal: Approximately 4 to 6 weeks through a notary.
Cost: Approximately €500 to €1,500 in notary and registration fees.

2.3 The Founding Mission Statement

The Hestia Foundation develops and disseminates open source construction
systems that make permanent, beautiful, affordable, and off-grid family housing
accessible to anyone with a few friends and a nail gun.

We build on the shoulders of giants — EPAL Euro pallets, CE-marked OSB, and
engineered I-joists are the infrastructure of global industry. We combine them
with ancient materials wisdom and modern assembly protocols to produce family
homes for €25,000 to €50,000 in materials. Our research is open source. Our
methodology is freely shared. Our mission is to demonstrate that the EU housing
crisis has an answer that is available today.

Positioning keywords for grant applications:
— Affordable modular housing
— Community-led construction
— Low-carbon building systems
— Circular construction materials
— Rural regeneration housing
— Off-grid residential systems
— Self-build empowerment
— Construction labour independence
— Innovative assembly-based housing
— Digital fabrication and protocol-driven construction

PART THREE: THE PROTOTYPE BUILD STRATEGY
================================================================================

3.1 The Prototype as the Foundation's First Asset

The single most important action the Hestia Foundation can take is to build one
complete house, document everything, and publish all of it.

Not a rendering. Not a CAD model. Not a thesis. A built, lived-in, measured,
photographed, sensor-equipped, structurally tested house.

This transforms the Foundation from a concept to a demonstrated technology. Every
grant application, every university partnership, every municipality conversation,
every media story becomes dramatically stronger with a real built house behind it.

The prototype build is the cornerstone of everything that follows.

3.2 What the Prototype Must Achieve

The prototype must be built to demonstrate:

STRUCTURAL PERFORMANCE:
— Wall panels loaded to document actual deflection
— Racking test: apply horizontal force to a complete wall assembly
— Thermal performance: continuous temperature logging inside and outside over
  one complete winter and one complete summer
— Compare measured U-values against calculated values in this thesis

COST DOCUMENTATION:
— Every receipt photographed and catalogued
— Total material cost verified against estimates in this thesis
— Labour time documented by role
— Professional fees documented

BUILD TIME DOCUMENTATION:
— Video documentation of complete build process
— Verified total assembly time from first earth screw to weathertight shell

SENSOR PERFORMANCE:
— Wall cavity humidity sensors logging from installation
— Smart home energy monitoring from move-in
— Hot water system performance over 12 months
— Solar PV production versus household consumption

PEST AND DURABILITY:
— Document salt installation in every panel base
— Photograph and verify moisture meter readings at 6 and 12 months
— Document whether any biological activity is detected in wall cavities

3.3 The Prototype as a Grant Application

A well-documented prototype build is itself a grant application. The NEB Boost
2026 (€30,000) and NEB Prizes 2026 (€20,000) can be applied for during or
immediately after the build. The prototype documentation package is the
application. No complex consortium required. No years of lead time.

The FCT research grant can be applied for to fund the university laboratory
testing of RCP panels — using the prototype's documented panels as the test
specimens. A Portuguese university partner converts the self-build documentation
into an academic research programme with institutional legitimacy.

3.4 The Documentation Package

The prototype generates the following deliverables, all publishable as
Foundation outputs:

— Complete photographic build record (every nail, every panel, every layer)
— Bill of materials with verified Portuguese retail prices
— Assembly time log by operation
— Structural test results
— Thermal performance data (12-month logged)
— Cost per square metre verified against market
— Comparison against conventional Portuguese construction cost
— Video documentary of the complete build (YouTube, educational)
— Academic paper submitted to peer-reviewed journal (Construction and Building
  Materials, Buildings, Sustainability)
— Open source protocol published on hestiafoundation.org

Each of these is a grant application asset. Each of them is also a media asset.
Each of them is also an educational asset for the workshop programme.

builds credibility and assets at each stage that unlock the next stage.

Target: Large consortium grants, EU-wide deployment, CE marking.

1. Apply for Horizon Europe Built4People partnership projects

Primary output: CE-marked system, EU-wide deployment, commercial operation.

PHASE FOUR (2030+):
Target: Pan-European Housing Investment Platform participation.

The EU's Pan-European Housing Investment Platform is launching now with a
mandate to fund scalable affordable housing systems. A Hestia Foundation with
a four-year track record, CE-marked technology, university validation, and
community deployment across multiple EU countries is exactly what this
platform is designed to fund at scale.

Estimated funding Phase Four: €20 million+ in investment and grants combined.

PART FIVE: THE PARTNERSHIP STRATEGY
================================================================================

5.1 Why Partners Are Essential

Grant applications without institutional partners are consistently scored lower.
The strongest applications show:
— A research partner (university or research institute)
— A deployment partner (municipality or housing authority)
— A technology partner (engineering firm or construction company)
— A social impact partner (NGO, housing charity, community organisation)

The Hestia Foundation needs to acquire these partnerships before the large
grant applications, not as a consequence of winning them.

5.2 Target Partners

UNIVERSITY PARTNERS (Portugal):
University of Algarve (UAlg) — Architecture and Civil Engineering departments
Instituto Superior Técnico (IST), Lisbon — structural engineering
LNEC (Laboratório Nacional de Engenharia Civil) — the national civil engineering
laboratory, which is the ideal partner for RCP panel certification testing

LNEC is the single most valuable partner available in Portugal. They have the
testing equipment, the institutional legitimacy, and the relationships with
planning authorities and engineers to make the RCP system formally certifiable
under Portuguese and EU standards. A joint research project between the Hestia
Foundation and LNEC converts a well-argued thesis into a certified building system.

MUNICIPALITY PARTNERS:
Municipality of Lagos — home territory, housing crisis is acute, rural setting
Municipality of Grândola — largest year-on-year price increase in Portugal
  (88.4% in 2025), significant housing need
Any municipality with rural depopulation challenge — the Alentejo interior,
Trás-os-Montes, or equivalent areas in Spain are natural targets

HOUSING AUTHORITY PARTNERS:
Instituto da Habitação e da Reabilitação Urbana (IHRU) — Portugal's national
housing and urban rehabilitation institute
Any social housing provider in Portugal or Spain

ENGINEERING PARTNERS:
A Portuguese licensed structural engineer willing to co-author the certification
submission is essential. This converts the Foundation's engineering analysis
into a stamped professional opinion.

5.3 How to Approach Partners

The prototype build is the approach tool. An invitation to the prototype
erection day — to watch a complete house go up in one to two days — is the
most compelling demonstration available. Nobody says no to watching that happen.

The sequence:
1. Build the prototype
2. Invite the target partner to the erection day
3. Show them the documentation package
4. Propose a research collaboration on the terms of their strongest interest
   (structural testing for the engineer, energy performance for the energy
   researcher, social impact for the municipality)
5. Formalise as a Letter of Intent before grant submission

The Hestia Foundation does not need to pay its partners. The grant funding
pays them. The partnership is the mechanism by which grant funding is obtained
and distributed. Partners contribute expertise, legitimacy, and institutional
capacity. The Foundation contributes the technology and the protocol.

PART SIX: THE LANGUAGE THAT WINS GRANTS
================================================================================

6.1 What EU Grant Reviewers Are Looking For

EU grant reviewers read hundreds of applications. The applications that score
highest share a consistent set of characteristics:

— They address a clearly stated EU policy priority using the EU's own language
— They demonstrate innovation beyond the current state of the art
— They show measurable social and environmental impact
— They involve credible institutional partners
— They have a realistic plan for dissemination and replication
— They do not sound like a business looking for free money

The Hestia Foundation's application language must be calibrated accordingly.

6.2 The Correct Framing — Phrase by Phrase

USE:                                    AVOID:
"Open source housing protocol"          "Our product"
"Community-empowered construction"      "DIY housing"
"Low-skill assembly system"             "Anyone can build it"
"Circular industrial materials"         "Pallets"
"Validated thermal performance"         "We think it's well insulated"
"Replicable at EU scale"               "We want to sell lots of houses"
"Carbon-negative building system"       "Eco-friendly"
"Reducing construction labour          "Cutting out the middleman"
  dependency"
"Social housing innovation"            "Cheap housing"
"Rural regeneration housing"           "Building in the countryside"
"Knowledge transfer programme"         "Teaching workshops"
"Living lab prototype"                 "Test house"

6.3 The Core Application Narrative

This should be the spine of every application, adapted to the specific call:

Europe needs 650,000 additional homes per year that are not being built.
The construction industry faces severe labour shortages — skilled workers are
scarce and the sector cannot scale to meet demand using conventional methods.
Meanwhile, the materials to build millions of homes are already circulating in
the European logistics network: 500 million EPAL Euro pallets in active
circulation, CE-marked OSB manufactured from timber industry offcuts, and
certified engineered I-joists available from any builders merchant.

The Hestia Foundation has developed an open source assembly protocol — the
Ribbed Composite Panel system — that enables permanent, insulated, off-grid
family homes to be assembled from these existing certified components by
organised non-specialist teams in one to two weeks, for €25,000 to €50,000
in materials. The system requires no concrete, no specialist trades beyond
brief certified professional interventions, and no equipment that does not fit
on a standard trailer.

This thesis constitutes the research foundation. The prototype constitutes the
demonstration. The university partnership constitutes the independent validation.
The community workshop programme constitutes the knowledge transfer mechanism.
The Foundation is seeking [grant name and amount] to fund [specific phase] in
order to [specific measurable outcome].

6.4 The Measurable Outcomes That Matter to Reviewers

Include specific, measurable outcomes in every application:

— Number of housing units enabled per €1 million of grant investment
  (significantly higher than any conventional approach)
— CO2 savings per house versus conventional construction
  (minimum 15 to 20 tonnes of embodied carbon avoided per house)
— Construction time reduction: 8 to 12 weeks conventional versus
  1 to 2 weeks RCP
— Cost per square metre: €277 to €437 versus €2,800 to €4,500 conventional
— Labour skill requirement reduction: skilled trades replaced by protocol
  following
— Communities trained per workshop cycle
— Open source publications generated
— Patent-free technology: freely replicable globally

PART SEVEN: THE PROTOTYPE LOCATION AND PLANNING
================================================================================

7.1 Algarve as the Prototype Location

Lagos, Algarve is the correct prototype location for multiple reasons:

Research relevance: The thesis is calibrated for Algarve conditions. Thermal
data, wind loads, solar data, soil conditions — all are Lagos-specific. The
prototype validates the thesis in exactly the conditions it was designed for.

Grant relevance: The Algarve is an EU Objective 2 region with access to
Portugal 2030 structural funds. Projects in this region receive preferential
scoring for regional development grants.

Housing crisis relevance: The Algarve's housing crisis is among the most
acute in Europe. Median property price at €3,203 per square metre in Q3 2025,
16.6 percent year-on-year increase. Documented, cited, directly relevant to
every grant application.

Climate data availability: PVGIS and LNEC climate databases provide
comprehensive data for the Lagos microclimate. All thermal calculations in
this thesis use verified Lagos data.

7.2 The Living Lab Framing

The prototype is not a show home. It is a living lab.

A living lab is a research concept the EU actively funds: a real-world built
environment where innovation is tested under genuine conditions of use. The
Hestia Foundation's prototype is a living lab for:

— Structural performance monitoring (sensor network from installation)
— Thermal performance monitoring (12-month logged data)
— Smart home energy optimisation (Shelly system data)
— Occupant experience research (qualitative)
— Maintenance performance (zero maintenance hypothesis tested)
— A Salty Pest Insurance effectiveness (moisture sensors in wall cavities)

The living lab framing converts a self-build project into a research facility.
It is the framing that unlocks university partnerships and EU research grants.

7.3 Permitting Strategy for the Prototype

The prototype should be permitted. The permit:

— Demonstrates that the system works within the conventional regulatory framework
— Makes the structure legally acceptable as a research facility
— Enables the university partnership (universities cannot partner on illegal
  structures)
— Provides the documentation that future applicants can reference
— Removes the most common objection raised by municipalities and engineers

The permit application for a lightweight timber frame on earth screws with
CE-marked structural components is more straightforward than most people
expect. The Hestia Foundation thesis is itself the supporting documentation.
A licensed Portuguese structural engineer (the Foundation's engineering partner)
stamps the structural calculations. A licensed architect stamps the drawings.
The municipality of Lagos processes the application.

Estimated time to permit: 3 to 6 months.
Estimated cost: €3,000 to €5,000 in professional fees and application charges.
