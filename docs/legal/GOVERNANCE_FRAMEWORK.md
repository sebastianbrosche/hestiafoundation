## Mission and Values

### Mission
To design, document, and distribute open-source construction systems that enable families to build durable, affordable, energy-independent homes using locally available materials.

### Core Values
1. **Openness** — All knowledge is shared. No patents, no secrets.
2. **Accessibility** — Housing is a human right, not a luxury.
3. **Sustainability** — Circular economy, low carbon, regenerative.
4. **Community** — Built by and for the people who live in it.
5. **Evidence** — Decisions based on data, not ideology.

## Membership

### Founding Members (3 minimum)
- Defined in the initial bylaws (`docs/legal/hestia_estatutos.md`)
- Rights: vote in General Assembly, elect Board, propose amendments
- Obligations: attend General Assembly, uphold values, pay no dues ( Associações typically have no membership fees)

### Ordinary Members
- Open to individuals and organisations who support the mission
- Application via email to Board, approved by simple majority
- Rights: attend General Assembly (non-voting), receive newsletter, volunteer
- No membership fees

### Honorary Members
- Recognised for exceptional contribution
- Nominated by Board, approved by General Assembly
- Same rights as ordinary members

## Governance Bodies

### 1. General Assembly (Assembleia Geral)
**Composition:** All founding and ordinary members
**Frequency:** Annual (minimum), extraordinary as needed
**Powers:**
- Elect and dismiss Board of Directors
- Amend bylaws (2/3 majority)
- Approve annual reports and accounts
- Dissolve the association (2/3 majority)
- Set strategic direction

**Quorum:** 50% of founding members + 20% of ordinary members
**Decisions:** Simple majority for most; 2/3 for bylaws and dissolution

### 2. Board of Directors (Direção)
**Composition:** 3–5 members elected by General Assembly for 2-year terms
**Current proposal:**
- **President:** Sebastian Brosche (founder, technical lead)
- **Vice-President:** [To be elected]
- **Treasurer:** [To be elected]
- **Secretary:** [To be elected]
- **Technical Director:** [To be elected — ideally engineer or architect]

**Powers:**
- Day-to-day management
- Financial management (within approved budget)
- Hiring and firing (staff, contractors)
- Partnership agreements (up to €10,000 value; larger needs Assembly approval)
- Grant applications (with Assembly notification)
- Public representation

**Meetings:** Monthly (minimum), quorum 3/5 members

### 3. Fiscal Council (Conselho Fiscal)
**Composition:** 3 members elected by General Assembly for 2-year terms
**Must include:** At least one member with accounting or auditing background
**Powers:**
- Review annual accounts
- Verify compliance with bylaws and law
- Report to General Assembly
- Investigate any suspected misuse of funds

**Meetings:** Quarterly

## Decision-Making Process

### Day-to-Day (€0–1,000)
- Board member decides individually
- Document in monthly report

### Operational (€1,000–10,000)
- Board vote (simple majority)
- Document in meeting minutes
- Report to General Assembly

### Strategic (>€10,000, new partnerships, bylaws)
- General Assembly vote
- 2-week notice required
- 2/3 majority for bylaws, simple majority for other strategic decisions

### Emergency
- President can act unilaterally up to €5,000 if urgent
- Must report to Board within 48 hours
- Must report to General Assembly at next meeting

## Financial Management

### Revenue Sources
1. EU and national grants (primary)
2. Private donations
3. Workshop fees (cost-recovery only)
4. Consulting fees (if relevant, reinvested in mission)
5. Merchandise (if any, cost-recovery only)

### Expenditure Categories
1. **Build and research** — Materials, tools, site costs, sensors (60–70%)
2. **Personnel** — Salaries, contractor fees (15–25%)
3. **Admin** — Legal, accounting, insurance, bank fees (5–10%)
4. **Dissemination** — Website, events, travel, publications (5–10%)
5. **Contingency** — Unforeseen costs (5–10%)

### Financial Controls
- Separate bank account in association's name
- Two signatures required for payments >€1,000
- Monthly financial review by Treasurer
- Quarterly review by Fiscal Council
- Annual audit (if revenue >€150,000 or required by grant)
- All transactions published in annual report

### Budget Process
1. Board drafts annual budget (November)
2. General Assembly approves (December)
3. Quarterly reviews and adjustments
4. Year-end reconciliation and report

## Conflict of Interest

**Definition:** Any situation where a Board or Assembly member's personal interests could conflict with Hestia's interests.

**Examples:**
- Board member's company supplies materials to Hestia
- Board member receives payment from Hestia (other than approved salary)
- Board member has financial stake in a partner organisation

**Procedure:**
1. Member declares conflict at meeting
2. Member recuses themselves from vote
3. Declaration recorded in minutes
4. If recurring or significant, General Assembly reviews

## Intellectual Property

**Policy:** All intellectual property created by or for Hestia Foundation is open-source.

**License:** CC BY-SA 4.0 (Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International)

**What this covers:**
- Build manuals and technical documentation
- CAD files, drawings, and designs
- Software, data visualisations, and tools
- Research data and publications
- Photos and videos (unless privacy restrictions apply)

**Exceptions:**
- Personal data of volunteers and partners (GDPR protected)
- Partner proprietary data (used with permission, not republished)
- Grant application materials (may have confidential partner info)

**Procedure for new IP:**
1. Mark with CC BY-SA 4.0 license on creation
2. Publish on GitHub within 30 days
3. Register copyright (optional) to prevent third-party patenting

## Amendment Process

**Bylaws amendment:**
1. Proposal by Board or 20% of founding members
2. 2-week notice to all members
3. General Assembly vote (2/3 majority)
4. Registration with Conservatória

**Governance framework amendment:**
1. Proposal by any member
2. Board discussion and recommendation
3. General Assembly vote (simple majority)
4. Publication on GitHub

---

## Dissolution

**Trigger:**
- General Assembly vote (2/3 majority)
- Court order
- Achievement of mission (if defined endpoint)

**Asset distribution:**
- All remaining assets transferred to another non-profit with similar mission
- No distribution to members or founders
- All IP remains open-source
- Records archived and published
