# How to Register a Non-Profit Association in Portugal

Last updated: 2026-05-29
Research source: Portuguese Justice Portal, IRN (Instituto dos Registos e do Notariado), Portuguese Association Law

---

## What You're Creating

**Legal form:** Associação (Association) — the most common non-profit form in Portugal
**Portuguese law:** Código Civil, Artigos 156º a 194º; Decreto-Lei n.º 119/99
**EU equivalent:** AISBL / Association internationale sans but lucratif

**Why an Associação:**
- No minimum capital required (unlike a company)
- Can be formed by 3+ people with any nationality
- Can apply for public funding, EU grants, and receive donations
- Tax exemptions available (Não Lucrativos regime, Mécénio / Patronage)
- Can employ staff, sign contracts, own property

---

## Requirements

### 1. Three Founding Members (minimum)

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Number** | Minimum 3 people |
| **Nationality** | Any nationality accepted |
| **Residency** | At least one member should have Portuguese NIF (Número de Identificação Fiscal) for tax purposes |
| **Age** | 18+ |
| **NIF needed** | Yes — every founder needs a Portuguese NIF (tax number) |
| **CRiminal record** | Clean criminal record certificate (not mandatory for registration but required for some public funding) |

**How to get a Portuguese NIF:**
- In person: Finanças office (Lagos, Portimão, Faro) or Loja do Cidadão
- Online: Portal das Finanças (requires Portuguese address and sometimes a fiscal representative)
- As a non-resident: You can get a NIF with a fiscal representative (any Portuguese resident or company)
- Time: Same day in person; 1–2 weeks online
- Cost: Free

**What you need:** Passport or EU ID card, proof of address (any country), fiscal representative contact (if non-resident)

### 2. Bylaws (Estatutos)

We have already drafted `hestia_estatutos.md`. You need to:
1. Review and customise the drafted bylaws
2. Have all 3 founding members sign them
3. The signature can be done in person or via notarised power of attorney

**Key clauses in the bylaws:**
- Name and headquarters (Lagos, Algarve)
- Purpose (open-source affordable housing research and development)
- Membership rules
- Governance structure (General Assembly, Board of Directors, Fiscal Council)
- Dissolution clause (assets transfer to similar non-profit)

### 3. Fiscal Representative (if any founder is non-resident)

If any of the 3 founders does not have a Portuguese address, they need a fiscal representative. Options:
- A Portuguese-resident founder can act as fiscal representative for all
- A Portuguese lawyer or accountant
- A professional fiscal representative company

## Step-by-Step Registration Process

### Step 1: Gather Founding Members (Week 1)
- Identify 3 people willing to be founding members
- Ensure at least one has or can get a Portuguese NIF
- If non-residents need NIFs, arrange fiscal representation
- Get clean criminal record certificates ( Certidão de Registo Criminal) from home country — optional but useful for future funding applications

### Step 2: Draft and Sign Bylaws (Week 2)
- Use `hestia_estatutos.md` as starting point
- Customise: names of founders, addresses, initial board composition
- All 3 founders sign in person or via notarised power of attorney
- If signing in person: any location works; signatures must be witnessed by a notary or lawyer (optional but recommended)

### Step 3: Open a Bank Account (Week 2–3)
- Portuguese bank account in the association's name
- Banks that accept non-profits: Millennium BCP, Caixa Geral de Depósitos, Novo Banco, Santander
- Required: signed bylaws, NIF of founders, proof of address, meeting minutes
- Initial deposit: €0 required (Associações have no minimum capital)
- Tip: Call ahead and ask specifically for "conta de associação sem fins lucrativos" — not all branches handle this

### Step 4: Register with the Conservatória do Registo Comercial (Week 3–4)

**Where:** Conservatória do Registo Comercial in Faro (for Algarve-based associations)
- Address: Conservatória do Registo Comercial de Faro, Rua de Portugal, 8000-269 Faro
- Or: Online via ePortugal (https://eportugal.gov.pt) → Registo de Pessoas Coletivas

**Documents required:**
1. Application form (Modelo 1 — available at ePortugal or the conservatória)
2. Signed bylaws (original + 2 copies)
3. Minutes of the founding meeting (signed by all founders)
4. NIF of all founding members
5. Proof of address of the association headquarters (rental contract or property deed)
6. Identification of all founders (passport or ID copies)
7. Declaration of acceptance of office by Board members (if different from founders)

**Cost:** €50–€100 (varies by conservatória)
**Time:** 2–4 weeks (in person); 4–6 weeks (online)

**What you get:**
- NIPC (Número de Identificação de Pessoa Coletiva) — the association's tax ID
- Registration certificate (Certidão de Registo Comercial)

### Step 5: Register with Finanças (Tax Authority) (Week 4–5)

**Where:** Portal das Finanças (online) or local Finanças office
**What to register:**
- NIPC activation
- Activity codes: CAE 94990 (Outras actividades de associações) or 94991 (Actividades de organizações associativas)
- Declare non-profit status (Não sujeição a IRC for eligible activities)

**Documents:** NIPC certificate, bylaws, bank account details

### Step 6: Apply for Non-Profit Tax Status (Optional but Recommended) (Month 2–3)

**Two regimes to consider:**

1. **Regime dos Não Lucrativos (RNL)** — Tax exemption for income related to statutory purpose
   - Apply to: Direção de Serviços de Benefícios Fiscais, Rua da Prata, 80, 1100-422 Lisboa
   - Or via Portal das Finanças → Modelo 22
   - Required: NIPC, bylaws, activity description, financial plan
   - Time: 3–6 months
   - Result: Exemption from IRC (corporate income tax) on mission-related income; donations may be tax-deductible for donors

2. **Mécénio / Statute of Patronage** — For receiving donations from individuals and companies
   - Apply to: Direção-Geral das Artes or relevant ministry
   - Benefit: Donors can deduct 25% (companies) or 25% (individuals) from tax
   - Required: 3 years of activity, audited accounts, proof of public benefit
   - Time: After 3 years of operation

### Step 7: Register with Social Security (if employing staff) (Month 3+)

- Only needed if you hire employees or pay contractors
- Register as employer at Segurança Social Direta (https://app.seg-social.pt)
- Required: NIPC, bank account, bylaws

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not getting NIFs first** — You need NIFs before you can register. Do this first.
2. **Wrong bank account** — Some banks reject non-profit accounts. Call ahead and confirm.
3. **Missing proof of address** — You need a Portuguese address for the association headquarters (can be a rental, office, or founder's home).
4. **Forgetting the fiscal representative** — Non-resident founders need one. Don't skip this.
5. **Not applying for RNL** — Without non-profit tax status, the association pays standard corporate tax (21% IRC). Apply for RNL as soon as possible.
