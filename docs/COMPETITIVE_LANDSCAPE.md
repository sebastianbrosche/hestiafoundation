- **Open-source** — No licensing, no corporate gatekeeping
- **Local materials** — Pallets available everywhere, no supply chain dependency

## Strategic Implications

### What This Means for Grants
- Hestia is not competing with existing social housing providers — it's enabling a new category
- We're not asking to replace traditional construction — we're offering a complementary system for specific contexts (rural, low-income, rapid deployment)
- The innovation is the **knowledge system**, not just the physical building

### What This Means for Partners
- Universities: Unique research opportunity in an unstudied field
- Municipalities: A tool they don't currently have
- NGOs: A system they can distribute freely
- Builders: A new service offering with low material costs

### What This Means for Critics
- "Pallet houses are just sheds" — No. Ours is engineered, insulated, weathertight, and monitored. Show us the data.
- "This already exists on YouTube" — Informal builds ≠ tested, documented, open-source system. Show us the peer-reviewed data.
- "It's too cheap to be safe" — LNEC testing + 12-month monitoring + Eurocode compliance will verify. Wait for the data.
- "People don't want to live in pallet houses" — We'll publish thermal comfort data, cost comparisons, and resident feedback. Judge after evidence.
