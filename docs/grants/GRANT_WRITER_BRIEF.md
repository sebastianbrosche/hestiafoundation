# Grant Writer Brief — Hestia Foundation

Last updated: 2026-05-29
For hiring a professional grant writer or briefing a volunteer

---

## About the Role

**Position:** Grant Writer / Proposal Manager (Freelance / Contractor)
**Duration:** June 2026 – December 2026 (with potential extension)
**Location:** Remote or Lisbon / Algarve, Portugal
**Fee:** €3,000–€6,000 for the full scope (negotiable based on experience)
**Payment:** 50% upfront, 50% upon submission of final HORIZON-NEB proposal

---

## Why We Need You

Hestia Foundation is a non-profit developing open-source affordable housing systems. We have:
- A strong concept (pallet-based construction, €25,000 homes)
- Verified partners (University of Algarve, LNEC)
- A live prototype planned for Q3 2026
- A website, GitHub repo, and growing media presence

What we lack:
- **EU grant writing experience** — specifically Horizon Europe and New European Bauhaus
- **Consortium coordination** — managing partners across institutions
- **Proposal polish** — translating technical concepts into fundable language

Your job is to turn our raw material into a winning proposal.

---

## Primary Objective

**Submit a competitive proposal to HORIZON-NEB-2026-01-REGEN-01 by 01/12/2026.**

This is a €12M total call, €4M per project, focused on:
- Regenerative neighbourhoods and communities
- Circular construction and sustainable materials
- Social innovation in housing
- Digital tools and open-source knowledge sharing

We believe Hestia is a strong fit. You will make that case.

## What We Need From You

### Phase 1: Strategy and Gap Analysis (Week 1–2, June)
- [ ] Review all Hestia documents and understand the project deeply
- [ ] Review HORIZON-NEB-2026-01-REGEN-01 call text in detail
- [ ] Identify gaps: missing partners, weak sections, compliance issues
- [ ] Propose consortium expansion (which additional partners would strengthen the bid?)
- [ ] Draft a 2-page strategy memo with recommendations

**Deliverable:** Strategy memo + partner wishlist

### Phase 2: Consortium Building (Week 3–6, June–July)
- [ ] Draft letters of support / intent for existing partners
- [ ] Identify and reach out to 3–5 additional potential partners
- [ ] Coordinate partner meetings (virtual) to align on roles and contributions
- [ ] Ensure each partner has a clear, fundable work package
- [ ] Collect CVs, capacity statements, and financial data from partners

**Deliverable:** Signed letters of support + partner agreements + consortium agreement draft

### Phase 3: Proposal Drafting (Week 7–14, July–September)
- [ ] Write the full proposal (typically 40–60 pages for Horizon Europe)
- [ ] Sections to cover:
  - **Excellence:** Innovation, methodology, open-source approach, state of the art
  - **Impact:** EU housing crisis alignment, NEB values, scalability, policy influence
  - **Implementation:** Work plan, Gantt chart, milestones, deliverables, risk management
  - **Consortium:** Partner roles, governance, financial breakdown
  - **Ethics, security, gender, open science:** Compliance sections
- [ ] Align with Horizon Europe template and evaluation criteria
- [ ] Ensure all mandatory annexes are complete (CVs, financial declarations, legal entities)

**Deliverable:** Complete first draft of proposal

### Phase 4: Review and Polish (Week 15–18, September–October)
- [ ] Internal review with Hestia team and key partners
- [ ] Incorporate feedback (2–3 revision rounds)
- [ ] External review (if possible — ex-evaluator or experienced grant writer)
- [ ] Final compliance check against call text
- [ ] Prepare submission package (Portal upload, PDFs, signatures)

**Deliverable:** Final proposal ready for submission

### Phase 5: Submission (Week 19–20, November–December)
- [ ] Submit via EU Funding & Tenders Portal
- [ ] Confirm receipt and completeness
- [ ] Prepare for possible evaluation queries (evaluators sometimes ask clarifications)
- [ ] Begin planning for NEB Boost and secondary applications

**Deliverable:** Submitted proposal + confirmation

### Phase 6: Secondary Applications (Ongoing, July–December)
- [ ] Adapt HORIZON-NEB material for NEB Boost (simpler, shorter)
- [ ] Adapt for NEB Facility, LIFE, Portugal 2030 as time permits
- [ ] Track deadlines and prepare submission calendar

**Deliverable:** 2–3 additional submitted applications

## Ideal Candidate Profile

**Must have:**
- Proven track record winning Horizon Europe or similar EU grants (H2020, LIFE, Erasmus+)
- Experience with New European Bauhaus, Horizon Europe Cluster 4/5/6, or NEB Facility
- Fluent English (Portuguese is a strong plus)
- Ability to translate technical/engineering concepts into fundable language
- Network of potential consortium partners in Portugal or EU
- Availability June–December 2026

**Nice to have:**
- Portuguese language
- Experience in construction, architecture, or civil engineering
- Understanding of open-source hardware / circular economy
- Experience with ANI (Portugal's NCP) or other NCPs
- Evaluator experience (ex-EC evaluator)
- Experience with non-profit / NGO funding

**What we don't need:**
- Someone who writes generic proposals from templates
- Someone who overpromises and underdelivers
- Someone who needs constant hand-holding
- Someone who treats this as just another job
