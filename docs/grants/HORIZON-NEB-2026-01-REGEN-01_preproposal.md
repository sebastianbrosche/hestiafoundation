## 1. Abstract (Max 2,000 characters)

The Hestia Foundation proposes a 36-month research and innovation programme to develop, test, and disseminate the Ribbed Composite Panel (RCP) — an open-source construction system that delivers thermally comfortable, affordable, off-grid family housing without concrete or structural steel. The RCP uses CE-marked timber I-joists, EPAL Euro pallets, and OSB sheathing to produce a weathertight, highly insulated building shell at approximately €25,000 for 37.5m². 

This project addresses the EU housing crisis directly: house prices have risen 60% since 2013, only 6–7% of housing stock is social housing, and 20% of homes remain unoccupied. The RCP system requires no mortgage, can be assembled by non-specialists in 5–10 days, and is optimised for the Mediterranean climate (Algarve, Portugal) with passive cooling, cross-ventilation, and evaporative cooling achieving comfortable indoor conditions without mechanical air conditioning.

The project will build two monitored prototypes, generate a 24-month thermal and structural performance dataset, produce engineer-certified documentation, and publish all protocols under Creative Commons. Consortium partners include a Portuguese university (structural verification), LNEC (materials testing), a Spanish university (thermal modelling), and a German research institute (life-cycle assessment).

## 2. State of the Art and Problem Statement

### 2.1 The EU Housing Crisis
- House prices risen 60% since 2013 (EU Commission data)
- Rents risen ~20% in same period
- Only 6–7% of EU housing stock is social housing
- 20% of homes remain unoccupied
- Short-term rentals surged 93% (2018–2024)
- Europe needs 2M+ homes per year
- Conventional construction cannot scale: skilled labour shortage, material cost inflation, carbon-intensive processes

### 2.2 Thermal Comfort Gap
- Mediterranean regions increasingly experience extreme heat events
- Mechanical air conditioning is energy-intensive and carbon-intensive
- Passive cooling solutions exist but are not integrated with affordable construction systems
- No peer-reviewed literature on pallet-based structural panels with integrated thermal performance

### 2.3 Existing Solutions and Gaps
| Solution | Cost | Build Time | Specialist Labour | Thermal Performance | Open Source |
|----------|------|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|-------------|
| Conventional masonry | €150K+ | 6–12 months | High | Variable | No |
| Prefab modular | €80K+ | 2–4 months | Medium | Good | No |
| Tiny house trailer | €40K+ | 1–2 months | Medium | Poor | Partial |
| Walter Segal self-build | €60K+ | 3–6 months | Medium | Variable | Partial |
| **RCP (proposed)** | **€25K** | **5–10 days** | **None** | **Verified** | **Yes** |

### 2.4 Innovation Opportunity
The RCP system represents a category shift: it combines structural performance, thermal comfort, affordability, and open-source accessibility in a single integrated system. No existing solution achieves all four simultaneously.

## 3. Objectives and Innovation

### 3.1 Overall Objective
Develop, verify, and disseminate an open-source construction system that delivers thermally comfortable, affordable, off-grid family housing for Mediterranean climates, with performance data verified by independent research institutions.

### 3.2 Specific Objectives

**SO1:** Complete structural verification of the RCP panel system under Eurocode 5, including load testing, connection testing, and long-term creep analysis. (Months 1–12)

**SO2:** Build and instrument two prototype dwellings (37.5m² and 84m²) in Algarve, Portugal, with continuous monitoring of thermal performance, energy consumption, structural deflection, and indoor air quality. (Months 3–18)

**SO3:** Validate passive cooling performance through 24-month monitoring, including summer peak temperature analysis, cross-ventilation effectiveness, and evaporative cooling contribution. (Months 6–30)

**SO4:** Conduct life-cycle assessment (LCA) comparing RCP against conventional masonry, prefab modular, and timber-frame construction on embodied carbon, operational energy, and end-of-life recyclability. (Months 12–24)

**SO5:** Develop and publish open-source construction protocols (text, video, CAD drawings) enabling non-specialist replication across EU Member States. (Months 6–36)

**SO6:** Disseminate results through peer-reviewed publications, conference presentations, workshop programmes, and policy briefs targeting national housing ministries and EU institutions. (Months 12–36)

### 3.3 Innovation
- **Structural:** First systematic study of EPAL pallet-timber I-joist composite panels under Eurocode 5
- **Thermal:** First integration of passive cooling protocols with a sub-€30K construction system
- **Process:** First open-source, fully documented, non-specialist-buildable housing system with independent verification
- **Material:** First use of industrial pallet waste stream as primary structural infill in permanent housing

## 4. Methodology and Work Programme

### WP1: Structural Engineering and Certification (Months 1–12)
- **Lead:** LNEC (Laboratório Nacional de Engenharia Civil, Portugal)
- **Tasks:**
  - T1.1: Review and verify load calculations (dead, live, wind, snow, seismic)
  - T1.2: Fabricate test panels and conduct destructive load testing
  - T1.3: Test connections (OSB-to-joist, panel-to-panel, panel-to-foundation)
  - T1.4: Long-term creep testing under sustained load
  - T1.5: Produce engineer-stamped structural drawings and calculation package
- **Deliverables:** D1.1 Structural test report; D1.2 Engineer-certified drawing set; D1.3 Peer-reviewed paper

### WP2: Prototype Construction and Monitoring (Months 3–30)
- **Lead:** Hestia Foundation (Portugal)
- **Partners:** University of Algarve (instrumentation), Municipality of Lagos (site)
- **Tasks:**
  - T2.1: Procure materials and prepare site (earth screw foundation)
  - T2.2: Build Shell A (37.5m²) with workshop team of non-specialists
  - T2.3: Build Shell B (84m²) with refined protocol
  - T2.4: Install sensor network (temperature, humidity, energy, structural deflection, indoor air quality)
  - T2.5: Conduct 24-month continuous monitoring with public data dashboard
  - T2.6: Seasonal thermal comfort surveys with occupants
- **Deliverables:** D2.1 Build protocol documentation; D2.2 Public monitoring dashboard; D2.3 24-month dataset; D2.4 Occupant comfort report

### WP3: Thermal Performance Validation (Months 6–30)
- **Lead:** [Spanish University — Thermal Modelling] (TBC)
- **Tasks:**
  - T3.1: Develop CFD model of cross-ventilation and stack effect
  - T3.2: Validate model against monitored data
  - T3.3: Optimise window placement and opening sizes for different orientations
  - T3.4: Test evaporative cooling effectiveness under varying humidity conditions
  - T3.5: Produce design guidelines for Mediterranean, Atlantic, and Continental climates
- **Deliverables:** D3.1 CFD validation report; D3.2 Climate-specific design guidelines; D3.3 Peer-reviewed paper

### WP4: Life-Cycle Assessment (Months 12–24)
- **Lead:** [German Research Institute — LCA] (TBC)
- **Tasks:**
  - T4.1: Compile material inventories for RCP, masonry, prefab, timber-frame benchmarks
  - T4.2: Calculate embodied carbon (A1–A3, C1–C4 per EN 15804)
  - T4.3: Model operational energy demand using monitored data
  - T4.4: Assess end-of-life recyclability and circularity potential
  - T4.5: Compare results across all four construction methods
- **Deliverables:** D4.1 LCA report; D4.2 EPD (Environmental Product Declaration) for RCP panel; D4.3 Policy brief

### WP5: Open-Source Dissemination (Months 6–36)
- **Lead:** Hestia Foundation
- **Tasks:**
  - T5.1: Produce step-by-step construction manual (text + photographs)
  - T5.2: Produce video documentation of full build process
  - T5.3: Release CAD drawings, cutting lists, and material specifications
  - T5.4: Develop workshop curriculum for non-specialist training
  - T5.5: Create online community platform for builder support
  - T5.6: Translate core documents into Portuguese, Spanish, French, German, Italian
- **Deliverables:** D5.1 Open-source construction manual; D5.2 Video library; D5.3 CAD package; D5.4 Workshop curriculum; D5.5 Community platform

### WP6: Exploitation and Policy Engagement (Months 12–36)
- **Lead:** Hestia Foundation + IHRU (Portugal)
- **Tasks:**
  - T6.1: Present results to Portuguese housing ministry (IHRU)
  - T6.2: Develop policy briefs for EU Member State housing ministries
  - T6.3: Engage with NEB Portugal National Hub and EU NEB Facility
  - T6.4: Coordinate with Habitat for Humanity and other NGOs for replication pilots
  - T6.5: Explore social housing procurement reform with municipal partners
- **Deliverables:** D6.1 Policy brief (EU); D6.2 Policy brief (Portugal); D6.3 Replication pilot agreements (2+)

## 5. Expected Results and Impact

### 5.1 Quantitative Targets
- **2** prototype dwellings built and monitored
- **24 months** continuous performance data
- **1** engineer-certified structural system
- **3+** peer-reviewed publications
- **5+** EU languages for core documentation
- **10+** replication pilots initiated across EU
- **€25,000** verified cost for 37.5m² weathertight shell

### 5.2 Impact Pathways
- **Environmental:** Avoid 5 tonnes CO₂e per dwelling vs. conventional masonry (embodied carbon reduction)
- **Social:** Enable median-income families to own housing without 30-year mortgage dependency
- **Economic:** Create local construction jobs through workshop model; reduce construction sector carbon intensity
- **Policy:** Provide verified data for EU affordable housing strategy; demonstrate alternative to conventional social housing procurement

### 5.3 NEB Values Alignment
- **Sustainability:** Zero concrete, zero steel, carbon-negative timber, upcycled industrial waste
- **Inclusivity:** Non-specialist buildable, mortgage-free, accessible to median incomes
- **Beauty:** Interior finishes (walnut, bronze, pine tar, engineered oak) that do not look like "affordable housing"
