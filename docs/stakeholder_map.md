## Engagement Strategies by Quadrant

### Key Players (High Power / High Interest)
**Strategy:** Involve directly, manage actively, satisfy needs
- Regular one-to-one meetings
- Decision-making roles where appropriate
- First access to information and opportunities
- Proactive issue resolution

### Keep Satisfied (Low Power / High Interest)
**Strategy:** Protect from negative impact, consult, inform
- Regular updates via newsletter
- Invitation to events and milestones
- Formal consultation on key decisions
- Transparent reporting

### Keep Informed (High Power / Low Interest)
**Strategy:** Monitor, keep informed, minimal engagement
- Quarterly summary reports
- Annual review meetings
- Escalation path for issues
- Clear communication channels

### Minimal Effort (Low Power / Low Interest)
**Strategy:** Monitor, maintain awareness, reactive engagement
- Website and public documentation
- Open office hours / Q&A
- Respond to inbound enquiries
- Include in general communications

## Conflict Resolution Protocol

### Potential Conflicts

1. **Founder vs. Board:** Strategic direction disagreement
   - Resolution: Pre-agreed decision-making framework; independent mediation if deadlocked

2. **University vs. Commercial:** IP ownership disputes
   - Resolution: Clear MOU terms; CC BY-SA 4.0 license; no exclusive IP

3. **Municipality vs. Community:** Planning objections
   - Resolution: Community consultation; transparent process; design adjustments

4. **Funders vs. Open Source:** Reporting requirements vs. openness
   - Resolution: Pre-agree publication rights; embargo periods if necessary

5. **Volunteers vs. Safety:** Risk-taking vs. caution
   - Resolution: Safety officer has veto power; no exceptions

### Escalation Path

```
Issue raised
    ↓
Team lead attempts resolution (48 hours)
    ↓
Project lead intervenes (72 hours)
    ↓
Steering committee decision (1 week)
    ↓
External mediation (if needed)
```
