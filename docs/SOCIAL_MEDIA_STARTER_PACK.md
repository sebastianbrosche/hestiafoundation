## Brand Voice

- **Direct, not corporate** — We build houses. We don't "deliver housing solutions."
- **Open, not guarded** — We share everything. Our failures are as interesting as our successes.
- **Confident, not arrogant** — We know this works. The data will prove it.
- **Urgent, not panicked** — Housing is a crisis. But crises are solvable.
- **Portuguese + English** — Primary content in English for EU/international reach. Portuguese for local audience.

**What we don't do:**
- No jargon without explanation
- No greenwashing or vague sustainability claims
- No polished marketing speak — real build photos, not stock images
- No begging for donations — we build, then we ask

## Content Pillars

### 1. The Build (40% of content)
- Step-by-step photos and videos
- "Day 3: walls are up" type updates
- Tool tips, mistakes, lessons learned
- Before/after transformations
- Drone footage of site (if available)

### 2. The Data (20% of content)
- Sensor readings and charts
- "After 30 days: interior temperature stays stable at 22°C despite 35°C outside"
- Cost breakdowns and comparisons
- "This wall costs €12 vs. €120 conventional"
- Energy performance graphs

### 3. The Crisis (15% of content)
- EU housing statistics (visual, shareable)
- Portugal-specific data
- "Since 2013, EU house prices up 60%. Our house costs €25,000."
- Policy commentary (New European Bauhaus, Horizon Europe)
- "What €4M in EU funding could build: 160 homes"

### 4. The Community (15% of content)
- Volunteer spotlights
- "Meet [Name], our lead carpenter from [Place]"
- University partnership announcements
- Partner organisation features (UAlg, LNEC, etc.)
- Event announcements (build workshops, open days)

### 5. The Philosophy (10% of content)
- Open-source manifesto
- "Why we're not patenting anything"
- Circular economy explainers
- "Housing is infrastructure, not a luxury"
- Quotes from team, partners, and supporters

## Visual Guidelines

**Photography:**
- Real build photos, not stock images
- Document the mess as well as the success
- Include people working (with consent)
- Before/after pairs are powerful
- Use natural light where possible

**Video:**
- Short-form (15–60 seconds) for Instagram Reels, TikTok, X
- Time-lapse sequences are highly shareable
- Voiceover explaining what we're doing and why
- No music required — ambient construction sounds are authentic

**Graphics:**
- Simple data visualisations (temperature charts, cost comparisons)
- One-pager infographics for sharing
- Use the website's colour palette (natural, earthy tones)
- No corporate stock-photo aesthetics

**Tone in visuals:**
- Hands-on, not polished
- Transparent, not performative
- The goal is "I could do that" not "I wish I could afford that"

## Engagement Rules

**Respond to:**
- Questions about the build process (within 24 hours)
- Offers to help or volunteer (within 48 hours)
- Media inquiries (within 4 hours)
- Criticism or scepticism (with data, not defensiveness)

**Don't respond to:**
- Trolls (block and move on)
- "This is illegal" comments without jurisdiction (unless they specify Portugal/EU)
- Offers to buy the "business" (we're not for sale, we're open-source)

**Amplify:**
- Partners posting about us (share, quote, thank)
- Volunteers sharing their experience (repost with credit)
- Academic publications citing our work (celebrate loudly)
- Media coverage (pin, share, thank)
