# Volunteer Recruitment Guide

Last updated: 2026-05-29
Target: Builders, students, professionals, and enthusiasts who want to help

---

## Why Volunteer With Hestia?

| What You Get | What You Give |
|--------------|---------------|
| Hands-on construction experience in a novel system | 5–10 days of active build time |
| Credit in open-source documentation and publications | Enthusiasm and willingness to learn |
| Networking with university researchers, engineers, and EU grant professionals | Your skills (any level welcome) |
| First-hand knowledge of a system that could change housing globally | Word-of-mouth to your network |
| Meals and accommodation during the build (if needed) | Participation in 12-month monitoring (optional) |
| Certificate of participation | Feedback to improve the build manual |

## Who We're Looking For

### 1. Build Team (5–6 people)
**When:** Q3 2026 (August–September), 10 active days
**Where:** Lagos area, Algarve, Portugal
**Skills needed:** Any of the following — carpentry, general construction, electrics, plumbing, or simply willingness to learn
**Experience level:** Mixed. We need 2 experienced builders + 4 learners.
**Commitment:** 10 days on-site, plus 2-hour safety briefing beforehand
**What you'll do:**
- Assemble pallet floor grid
- Build and lift wall panels
- Install roof structure
- Run electrics and plumbing
- Install insulation and interior finishes
- Document the process with photos and notes

### 2. Student Researchers (2–3 people)
**When:** September 2026 – ongoing
**Where:** University of Algarve + Lagos site
**Skills needed:** Civil engineering, architecture, environmental science, or data analysis
**Experience level:** MSc or PhD student preferred; undergrads with strong motivation considered
**Commitment:** 3–6 months part-time, including 2–3 site visits
**What you'll do:**
- Structural monitoring (sensor data analysis)
- Thermal performance analysis
- Moisture and durability tracking
- Co-author peer-reviewed publications
- Potential thesis topic: "Performance of Pallet-Based Composite Panels in Mediterranean Climate"

### 3. Documentation Team (2 people)
**When:** Ongoing, remote
**Where:** Anywhere (remote)
**Skills needed:** Technical writing, photography, video editing, web design, or translation
**Experience level:** Any
**Commitment:** 2–5 hours/week, flexible
**What you'll do:**
- Improve build manuals with clearer photos and diagrams
- Translate documents into Portuguese, Spanish, French, or other languages
- Edit video footage from the build
- Maintain the website and GitHub repo
- Create social media content

### 4. Grant Writing Support (1 person)
**When:** June–December 2026
**Where:** Remote or Lisbon/Algarve
**Skills needed:** EU grant writing experience, Horizon Europe or NEB familiarity
**Experience level:** Professional or experienced volunteer
**Commitment:** 20–40 hours total, spread over 6 months
**What you'll do:**
- Review and strengthen HORIZON-NEB-2026-01-REGEN-01 proposal
- Help with NEB Boost application
- Research additional funding opportunities
- Liaise with ANI (National Contact Point) for free advisory

### 5. Local Networkers (2–3 people)
**When:** Ongoing
**Where:** Algarve region, Portugal
**Skills needed:** Portuguese language, local connections, community organising
**Experience level:** Any
**Commitment:** 1–2 hours/week
**What you'll do:**
- Connect us with local builders, suppliers, and landowners
- Help with Portuguese-language communications (municipality, permits, suppliers)
- Spread the word in local communities
- Organise community info sessions or build demo days

## How to Apply

**Email:** info@hestiafoundation.org
**Subject:** "Volunteer Application — [Role]"
**Include:**
- Your name and location
- Which role you're interested in
- Relevant skills or experience (none required for build team)
- Availability (dates for build team, hours/week for ongoing roles)
- Why you want to be involved (1–2 sentences)
- CV or portfolio (optional, for professional roles)

**What happens next:**
1. We review applications within 1 week
2. Short video call (15 minutes) to confirm fit and answer questions
3. For build team: safety briefing and role assignment
4. For remote roles: onboarding to GitHub, Slack/Discord, or email workflow

## Safety and Accommodation

**Safety:**
- Mandatory 2-hour safety briefing before build starts
- First aid kit on site at all times
- No work at height without harness or scaffold
- Power tools only used by trained volunteers or under supervision
- Insurance: volunteers must have their own health/travel insurance

**Accommodation:**
- For build team: we can arrange basic accommodation (shared house, camping, or local hostel) at no cost to volunteers
- Meals: provided during build days (simple, hearty, local food)
- Transport: volunteers arrange their own travel to Lagos; we provide transport from accommodation to site

**What to bring:**
- Work clothes and boots (steel toe cap recommended)
- Sun protection (hat, sunscreen, sunglasses)
- Water bottle (refills provided)
- Personal tools (optional — we provide main tools)
- Camera or phone for documentation

---

## Code of Conduct

1. **Respect** — Treat everyone with respect regardless of skill level, background, or identity
2. **Safety** — Never compromise on safety for speed
3. **Documentation** — If you see something interesting, photograph it and note it
4. **Openness** — All work is open-source. Share knowledge freely.
5. **Sustainability** — Minimise waste, reuse materials, respect the site

## Recognition
