### EUROPE'S FIRST OPEN-SOURCE, MORTGAGE-FREE FAMILY HOME BUILT IN 10 DAYS FOR €38,000

**Lagos, Algarve, Portugal** — The Hestia Foundation has completed construction of a 37.5m² family home using the Ribbed Composite Panel (RCP), an open-source construction system made from timber I-joists, EPAL Euro pallets, and OSB sheathing. The prototype was built in 10 days by a team of non-specialist volunteers at a total cost of €38,180 — including off-grid solar power, hot water, and composting toilet.

"This is not a concept. This is a house you can live in, built by people who had never used a nail gun before," said [Name], [Title] of the Hestia Foundation. "The question was never whether we could build a cheap house. The question was whether we could build a beautiful, permanent, thermally efficient home that a median-income family could own outright without a 30-year mortgage. We have proven that we can."

**Key facts:**
- **Cost:** €38,180 complete (materials + services + off-grid systems)
- **Build time:** 10 days weathertight, 15 days complete
- **Team:** 8 volunteers, no specialist construction skills
- **Materials:** CE-marked timber I-joists, EPAL Euro pallets, 12mm OSB sheathing
- **Foundation:** Earth screws — no concrete, no steel
- **Energy:** 3.64kW solar PV, 10kWh battery, solar hot water, composting toilet
- **Thermal:** Passive cooling, cross-ventilation, evaporative cooling — no air conditioning needed in Algarve climate
- **Open source:** All plans, protocols, and data published under CC BY-SA 4.0

The prototype will be continuously monitored for 12 months, with temperature, humidity, energy consumption, and structural deflection data published live to a public dashboard. The Foundation will publish a complete build protocol with verified costs, enabling replication across Europe.

**About the Hestia Foundation**
The Hestia Foundation is a Portuguese non-profit organisation developing and disseminating open-source construction systems for affordable, permanent, off-grid family housing. Based in Lagos, Algarve, the Foundation operates under a dual structure: the non-profit holds intellectual property and research data, while Hestia Build executes construction and workshops.
