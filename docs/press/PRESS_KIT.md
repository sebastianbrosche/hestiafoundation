## About Hestia Foundation

**Mission:** Open-source construction systems for affordable, permanent, off-grid family housing.
**Location:** Lagos, Algarve, Portugal
**Website:** https://hestiafoundation.org
**GitHub:** https://github.com/sebastianbrosche/hestiafoundation
**Contact:** info@hestiafoundation.org

**The Problem:** Since 2013, EU house prices have risen over 60%. Portugal faces acute housing pressure. The European Commission has mobilised €43 billion for affordable housing (2021–2027), yet 6–7% of EU housing stock is social housing — among the lowest rates globally.

**The Solution:** The Ribbed Composite Panel (RCP) — a building system using standard EPAL Euro pallets (€8–12 each) as the primary structural element. A 37.5 m² weathertight home costs approximately €25,000, can be assembled by non-specialists in 5–10 days, and requires no concrete or structural steel.

**The Innovation:** Open-source everything. All designs, cost data, build protocols, and sensor data are published under CC BY-SA 4.0. Anyone can download, modify, and build.

**On the prototype:**

## Story Angles

### 1. The Housing Crisis Solution
- EU house prices up 60%, rents up 20%
- Portugal up 94% — 4th highest in EU
- 20% of EU housing stock is unoccupied while 6–7% is social housing
- Hestia offers a €25,000 home vs. €150,000+ conventional

### 2. The Open-Source Hardware Movement
- Like Arduino for electronics, Hestia is for housing
- CC BY-SA 4.0 license — anyone can build, modify, sell
- No patents, no licensing fees, no corporate gatekeeping
- GitHub repo with full CAD, BOM, and build videos

### 3. The Circular Economy
- EPAL pallets are a waste stream (2.5 billion circulate in EU annually)
- Each home reuses 80–100 pallets that would otherwise be discarded or recycled at cost
- No concrete = no cement industry carbon (8% of global CO₂)
- No structural steel = no mining, no smelting

### 4. The EU Grant Race
- Portugal has 39.3% Horizon Europe success rate (2.5x EU average)
- Hestia targets HORIZON-NEB-2026-01-REGEN-01 (€4M per project, deadline 01/12/2026)
- Also targeting NEB Boost (€30K), NEB Facility, LIFE Programme, Portugal 2030
- ANI (national innovation agency) provides free advisory

### 5. The Student Build
- 10-day hands-on workshop format
- University of Algarve civil engineering students involved in structural monitoring
- LNEC (National Laboratory for Civil Engineering) providing independent testing
- Real-world thesis opportunities in a field with zero peer-reviewed literature

### 6. The Climate Resilience Angle
- Algarve = hot, dry summers, mild winters, occasional storms
- Sensor network monitors thermal performance, humidity, structural deflection
- 12-month data collection = first empirical evidence on pallet construction durability
- Applicable to Mediterranean, North African, and similar climates globally
