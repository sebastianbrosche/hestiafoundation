The Ribbed Composite Panel

How to build a weathertight, insulated, permanent home using standard EPAL Euro pallets as the primary structural element.

Overview

The Ribbed Composite Panel (RCP) is a building system that uses standard EPAL Euro pallets (1200 × 800 mm, rated 1500 kg dynamic load) arranged in interlocking grids, reinforced with engineered I-joists, and sheathed with OSB or cement-board. The result is an 84 m² building shell that costs approximately €20,000 in materials and labour, can be assembled by non-specialists in 14 active days, and requires no concrete or structural steel.

Core Components

🪵

EPAL Euro Pallets

180–220 standard 1200×800 mm pallets per 84 m² module. Interlocked in grids to form floor, wall, and roof structures. Rated 1500 kg dynamic load each.

⚙️

I-Joist Reinforcement

Engineered timber I-joists reinforce pallet grids, providing structural continuity, racking resistance, and support for roof beams.

🛡️

OSB / Cement-Board Sheathing

12 mm OSB or cement-board sheathing creates a rigid diaphragm, provides weather protection, and forms the substrate for insulation and interior finishes.

🧊

Mineral Wool Insulation

100–150 mm mineral wool in walls, roof, and floor cavities. Non-combustible, moisture-resistant, and recyclable.

🌧️

Breathable Membrane + Roofing

Waterproof breathable membrane under Onduline bitumen sheets, corrugated metal, or tiles. Protects structure while allowing vapour escape.

🔩

Ground Screw Foundation

12–16 adjustable ground screws (or screw piles) replace concrete foundations. Removable, reusable, minimal soil disturbance.

Structural Engineering

The RCP system is being reviewed by a Portuguese-licensed structural engineer for compliance with:

  • Eurocode 1: Wind loads (Algarve design wind speed)
  • Eurocode 5: Timber structures (I-joist and pallet wood properties)
  • Eurocode 8: Seismic design (Portugal Zone 1.3/1.4)
  • Eurocode 6: Masonry (not applicable — RCP replaces masonry)

Independent testing by LNEC (National Laboratory for Civil Engineering) will verify panel racking resistance, compressive capacity, and connection strength.

Build Sequence (10 Days)

Day 1

Foundation + Floor

Install ground screws, lay pallet floor grid (7×12 m), secure and level, install OSB subfloor.

Day 2–3

Wall Panels — Prefabrication

Cut I-joists, assemble wall frames, attach OSB sheathing, install insulation, label panels.

Day 4

Wall Erection

Lift and secure all 4 wall panels, brace, check plumb, connect to floor frame.

Day 5

Roof Structure

Install roof beams, pallet grid, roof sheathing, membrane, and roofing material.

Day 6

Windows, Doors, Sealing

Install windows and door, seal all joints with foam/tape, external membrane, weathertight.

Day 7

Electrics

Run cable, install breaker box, sockets, switches, LED lights. Test circuits.

Day 8

Plumbing

Install water tank, run PEX pipe, connect sink/shower, test pressure and drainage.

Day 9

Insulation + Interior

Fill gaps, install internal wall/ceiling lining, floor finish, seal joints.

Day 10

Systems + Testing

Install solar (if off-grid), test all systems, check for drafts, thermal camera scan.

Day 11–12

Interior Fit-Out

Install kitchen, bathroom, storage, sleeping area. Paint and seal surfaces.

Day 13–14

Handover + Monitoring

Document everything, install 20+ sensors, calibrate, begin 12-month data collection.

Primary metric: € per m². The goal is a finished house that costs no more per m² than the land it stands on. In non-central Algarve, buildable land averages ~€100/m². Sustainability

  • No concrete: Cement industry = 8% of global CO₂. Eliminated.
  • No structural steel: Mining and smelting eliminated.
    • Circular materials: 2.5 billion pallets circulate in the EU. Reusing 80–100 per home turns waste into resource.
  • Local sourcing: Pallets available within 50 km of almost any EU location. No long-distance transport.
  • Recyclable: At end of life, pallets become mulch or biomass. Mineral wool is recyclable. OSB is biodegradable.
  • Embodied carbon target: <200 kg CO₂e/m² vs. 300–500 for conventional construction.
