The Problem

Housing has become a financial instrument.
People need a home.

In the 1970s, two years of average salary bought a home in Sweden. Today it takes eight. In Portugal, where we are building, it takes nineteen years of gross income.

The house has not changed. The system has. Property is no longer shelter. It is an asset class, bought by funds, held for yield, and priced beyond the reach of anyone who simply needs a place to live.

A home should not require a debt sentence. A family should not hand the majority of their working life to a lender to afford four walls and a roof. A bank does not build a house. A family does. Yet the bank receives more years of a family's labour than the family receives years in their home.

The Solution

Five components. Two tools. One protocol.

The Hestia Ribbed Composite Panel (RCP) replaces the petroleum foam core of a standard SIP with a timber skeleton that Europe already produces in unlimited quantity. Lightweight, modular, reversible, and over-engineered to last 100 years.

EPAL Euro Pallet

€8-12. Any logistics depot in Europe. The most standardized industrial object on the continent. 500 million in circulation.

Structural core

OSB3 Board

€12-16 per sheet. Any builders merchant. Two 15mm skins bonded to the pallet create a composite panel stronger than conventional SIP.

Structural skin

I-Joist

€18-24/m. Any timber merchant. Used in 80%+ of new timber frame construction in Northern Europe. Lighter, straighter, longer span.

Roof/floor spanning

Helical Ground Screw

€30-50 installed. Foundation in 3-4 hours, not 3-4 weeks. No excavation, no concrete, no curing, no weather dependency.

Foundation

PU Adhesive + Nails

€0.50/m² panel. Bond the composite. Ring-shank nails provide mechanical backup. The bond is stronger than the foam it replaces.

Bonding system

The Result

Shear strength: 20-50x EPS. Screw pull-out: 10-20x foam. Carbon: negative (timber stores carbon). End of life: compostable or recyclable. No concrete. No rebar. Over-engineered.

Speed

From delivery to weathertight in under two weeks

Everything slow happens before assembly day. Assembly day is pure connection. Four standard components, two tools, one protocol. No concrete. No rebar. No curing.

Foundation

3-4h

12-16 ground screws. Handheld driver. Any weather. Immediate load-bearing.

Wall Panels

2 days

4 people, one panel every 10-15 minutes. Bolted connections. No crane.

Roof Structure

3 days

I-joists drop into hangers. Purlins, steel roofing, sealed.

Electrical

4-6h

Cables through pallet voids. No drilling. No chasing.

Insulation

2.5h

Blown rock wool. 634 kg fills entire home. Professional contractor.

Total

< 2 weeks

From first panel lift to weathertight seal. No concrete curing. No weather delays. Over-engineered. Reversible. Plop in place and stay 100 years.

Traction & Roadmap

Prototype build: Algarve, Q3 2026

This is not a demonstration. It is a test. The 37.5m² Shell A module will be instrumented, monitored for 12 months, and all data published openly. Inspired by WikiHouse, who proved that open-source building systems can generate real community momentum through technical transparency.

Q3 2026

Prototype Build

37.5m² module in Algarve. Full sensor network. Live data dashboard.

Q3 2026

Structural Testing

LNEC: compression, shear, racking, fire. Publish all results.

Q4 2026

Code Approval

Municipal permit under "innovative system" pathway. Engineer stamp.

Q1 2027

First Workshop

10-day self-build training. 10 participants. Open-source manual.

Q2 2027

First Replication

Independent build by trained participants. Verify system transferability.
