Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about building, funding, partnering, and getting involved.

The Building System

How much does it cost to build a Shell A? +

Materials only: approximately €10,000–15,000 for a 37.5 m² weathertight shell. With electrics, plumbing, insulation, and interior finishes: €15,000–20,000. With labour costs: €20,000–25,000. With volunteer labour and bulk sourcing, €10,000–15,000 is realistic for the basic shell.

How long does it take to build? +

10 active build days with a team of 4–6 people. Spread across 2–4 weeks including drying/curing time and rest days.

Do I need specialist skills? +

No specialist construction skills required. Basic carpentry knowledge helps but is not essential. We provide a complete build manual with step-by-step photos and videos. We recommend having one experienced builder on the team for safety and quality.

Are the plans really free? +

Yes. All designs, build manuals, CAD files, cost data, and performance data are published under CC BY-SA 4.0. You can download, modify, build, and even sell your modifications — as long as you share your improvements under the same license.

Is it safe? +

EPAL pallets are rated for 1,500 kg dynamic load. Our system uses 80–100 pallets in a redundant grid with I-joist reinforcement and OSB sheathing. The prototype will be independently tested by LNEC (National Laboratory for Civil Engineering) and reviewed by a Portuguese-licensed structural engineer.

Is it warm in winter and cool in summer? +

Shell A uses 100–150 mm mineral wool insulation in walls, roof, and floor. This provides U-values of 0.25–0.35 W/m²K — comparable to conventional Portuguese timber frame construction. The 12-month sensor monitoring will provide empirical data.

What about pests (termites, rodents)? +

The prototype uses heat-treated (HT) pallets (marked "HT" on the pallet) which are free of chemicals and less attractive to pests than raw timber. Regular inspection and maintenance are recommended. Ground screws raise the structure off the ground, reducing pest access.

Materials & Sourcing

Where do I get EPAL pallets? +

EPAL pallets are widely available across Europe. In Portugal, check local logistics companies, pallet recyclers, OLX.pt, Facebook Marketplace, or direct from EPAL-licensed manufacturers. Typical cost: €8–12 per used pallet in good condition. For bulk orders (100+), negotiate down to €6–8.

What condition do the pallets need to be in? +

Structurally sound: no broken stringers, no rot, no chemical contamination. Look for "EPAL" and "HT" (heat-treated) marks. Avoid pallets with spills, stains, or unusual odours.

Can I use other types of pallets? +

The build manual is designed for EPAL Euro pallets (1200×800 mm). Other sizes can be adapted but require modifications to the plans.

Legal & Open Source

What license do you use? +

CC BY-SA 4.0 (Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International). You can use the designs for any purpose, including commercial builds, modify and adapt them, and share your modifications. You must credit Hestia Foundation and share your modifications under the same license.

Can I sell houses built with your system? +

Yes. You can build and sell pallet houses using our plans. You must credit Hestia Foundation and share any improvements you make to the system.

Do I need a building permit? +

In Portugal, structures under 50 m² with temporary or lightweight foundations may not require a full building permit. However, you must check with your local Câmara Municipal (municipality). We are working with Lagos Municipality to clarify requirements.

Who is liable if something goes wrong? +

Hestia Foundation provides open-source documentation for educational purposes. We are not a construction company and do not assume liability for individual builds. Builders are responsible for compliance with local building codes, engaging qualified professionals where required, safe construction practices, and structural verification for their specific site and climate.

Getting Involved

Can I help build the prototype? +

Yes! We need 4–6 build team members for Q3 2026. No experience required — enthusiasm and willingness to learn are enough. We provide meals during build days and can arrange accommodation at no cost. You arrange your own travel to Lagos, Portugal.

Can I do a thesis on this? +

Yes. We welcome student researchers from civil engineering, architecture, environmental science, and data analysis. Potential thesis topics include structural performance of pallet-based composite panels, thermal behaviour in Mediterranean climate, life cycle assessment (LCA) of pallet construction, and social acceptance of alternative building systems.

Can my university partner with you? +

Yes. We are actively seeking partnerships with University of Algarve and other institutions. Partners get co-authorship on publications, funded work packages in EU grant applications, and access to 12-month sensor datasets.

How do I donate? +

Donations will be accepted once our non-profit registration and bank account are complete. Check back here for updates or contact us.

Project Status

When is the prototype being built? +

Q3 2026 (August–September) in the Lagos area, Algarve, Portugal. Exact dates depend on site confirmation, material delivery, and team availability.

What grants are you applying for? +

Our primary target is HORIZON-NEB-2026-01-REGEN-01 (€4M, deadline 01/12/2026). We are also preparing NEB Boost (€30K), NEB Facility, LIFE Programme, and Portugal 2030 applications.

Where are you based? +
