Get Involved

We're a non-profit building open-source housing knowledge. We need builders, researchers, partners, and funders. Join us.

Volunteer for the Build

We need 4–6 people to help build Shell A in Algarve, Portugal, over 10 active days in Q3 2026 (August–September). No experience required — enthusiasm and willingness to learn are enough.

  • When: Q3 2026, 10 active days (spread across 2–4 weeks)
  • Where: Lagos area, Algarve, Portugal
  • Who we need: Mixed team — 2 experienced builders + 4 learners. Any of: carpentry, general construction, electrics, plumbing, or simply willingness to learn.
  • Cost to you: None. We provide meals during build days and can arrange basic accommodation at no cost. You arrange your own travel to Lagos.
  • What you get: Hands-on construction experience, credit in open-source documentation, certificate of participation, networking with university researchers and EU grant professionals.

Apply: info@hestiafoundation.org with subject "Volunteer Application — Build Team". Include your name, location, relevant skills (none required), availability, and why you want to be involved (1–2 sentences).

Student Researchers

We welcome MSc and PhD students from civil engineering, architecture, environmental science, and data analysis.

  • Duration: 3–6 months part-time, including 2–3 site visits
  • Potential thesis topics: Structural performance of pallet-based composite panels; thermal behaviour in Mediterranean climate; life cycle assessment (LCA); social acceptance of alternative building systems
  • What you get: Co-authorship on publications, access to 12-month sensor dataset, real-world thesis with zero existing literature

Apply: info@hestiafoundation.org with subject "Student Research Application".

Partner With Us

We are actively seeking institutional partners for the prototype build, EU grant consortium, and long-term scaling.

  • Universities: Civil engineering faculty, architecture schools, environmental science departments. We need letters of support for EU grant applications and student research supervision.
  • Research labs: Structural testing, material science, thermal performance analysis. LNEC (National Laboratory for Civil Engineering) is already engaged.
  • Municipalities: Land access, permitting guidance, community engagement. We're working with Lagos Municipality.
  • Structural engineers: Licensed Portuguese engineers to review calculations and sign off on documentation.
  • Material suppliers: Pallet recyclers, builders merchants, insulation manufacturers. Named supplier in all documentation.

Fund the Project

We need seed funding to build the prototype and register the non-profit. This enables us to apply for €4M in EU grants — a 1:1000 leverage ratio.

  • Prototype build: €10,000–15,000 (materials, tools, sensors)
  • Legal registration: €200–400 (Portuguese non-profit)
  • Grant writer: €3,000–6,000 (HORIZON-NEB proposal)
  • Total seed: €15,000–25,000

Donations will be accepted once our non-profit registration and bank account are complete. For immediate partnership or grant enquiries: info@hestiafoundation.org.

Contribute Documentation

Not in Portugal? Contribute remotely:

  • Technical writing: Improve build manuals with clearer photos and diagrams
  • Translation: Portuguese, Spanish, French, German — or any language where affordable housing is needed
  • Video editing: Edit build footage into tutorials
  • Web development: Sensor dashboards, cost calculators, mobile apps
  • Design: CAD improvements, interior layouts, climate adaptations

All contributions are tracked on GitHub. See our Contributing Guide for details.
