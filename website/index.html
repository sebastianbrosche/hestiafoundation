01 — The Problem

Why housing became unaffordable

1

The concrete trap

Modern construction is addicted to concrete — 8% of global CO₂, requiring specialist labor, heavy machinery, and months of curing. A concrete foundation alone costs €15,000-30,000 before you've built a single wall.

2

The labor monopoly

Building a house requires 12+ different trades, each with their own pricing power, scheduling conflicts, and markup chains. Labor becomes 50-70% of total cost. Self-build is technically illegal in many jurisdictions without certification.

3

The proprietary lock-in

Every "innovative" building system is patented, licensed, and controlled. You can't modify it. You can't improve it. You can't afford it. The profit model requires scarcity.

4

The scale fallacy

Industrial housing promises economies of scale but delivers identical boxes, transported by diesel trucks, dependent on global supply chains. A factory 500km away is not local. A module on a truck is not agile.

"The problem of architecture has always been the problem of housing the greatest number of people at the least cost with the greatest dignity. We have the technology. What we lack is the system."
— after Buckminster Fuller, via Hestia

System Logic

01

EPAL pallets as structural lattice

Standard 1200×800mm Euro pallets interlock in alternating orientations, creating a rigid 2D grid. The existing pallet structure — three stringers, deck boards, blocks — becomes the wall frame. No cutting required for standard modules.

02

I-joist reinforcement

Engineered wood I-joists slot through pallet voids, creating continuous vertical and horizontal spines. This transforms the grid from a collection of boxes into a monocoque structure.

03

OSB sheathing as diaphragm

18mm OSB panels screw to both faces of the pallet grid, creating shear diaphragms. The wall becomes a stressed-skin panel — rigid in-plane, insulated in-section.

04

Mineral wool infill

140mm rock wool fills the 145mm cavity between OSB faces. Thermal bridge-free. Fire-resistant. Sound-absorbing. Breathable.

05 — Assembly

10 days. 4 people. 1 house.

IKEA-style protocol. No prior construction experience required.

D1

Site Prep & Foundation

Level ground. Lay 12 precast concrete pads (€8 each). No digging, no concrete pour, no curing time. Assemble first course of pallets directly on pads.

Pads: €96 | Time: 6 hours | Team: 4 people
D2

Wall Assembly — Course 1-2

Interlock EPAL pallets in alternating orientations. Slot I-joists through stringer voids. Screw OSB to exterior face. Add insulation batts.

Pallets: 40 | Time: 8 hours
D3

Wall Assembly — Course 3-4

Continue walls to full height (2.4m). Cut window/door openings with circular saw. Install lintels (doubled I-joists).

Openings: 4 | Time: 8 hours
D4

Roof Structure

Assemble roof grid from pallets laid flat. I-joist ridge beam. OSB roof deck. Creates 300mm cavity for extra insulation.

Roof pitch: 15° | Time: 8 hours
D5

Weatherproofing

Breather membrane to walls. Roofing membrane + battens to roof. Window and door frames installed. House is now weathertight.

Membrane: 2 rolls | Time: 8 hours
D6-D7

Electrics & Plumbing Rough-in

Cable runs through pallet voids (natural conduit). Plumbing via flexible PEX. Solar panel mounting prep on roof. No chasing, no concrete cores.

Cable: 150m | PEX: 40m | Time: 2 days
D8-D9

Interior Finishes

OSB interior face as finished surface (painted) or gypsum board overlay. Floor: OSB + vinyl or laminate. Kitchenette, bathroom pod, storage.

Interior: €5,000-8,000 | Time: 2 days
D10

Systems Commissioning

Solar panels connected. Water heater test. LED lighting circuits. Sensor network installation (temperature, humidity, structural). Handover.

Sensors: 20+ | Time: 6 hours

07 — Logistics

The math that makes it scalable

Supply Chain

2.5 billion EPAL pallets circulate in the EU annually. That's 5,000 pallets per potential home, every year.
€8-12 Per pallet, anywhere in Europe. No special orders. No freight from China. Every town has a pallet supplier.
1 truck Delivers all materials for one 37.5m² shell. 90 pallets + I-joists + OSB sheets + insulation. One trip.

Labor Model

320 hours Total labor for 37.5m² shell. 4 people × 10 days × 8 hours. No specialists. No certifications.
€0 Labor cost at self-build. Family, friends, community. The protocol is the skill transfer.
4 tools Circular saw, drill/driver, level, hammer. Under €400 total. Available at any hardware store.

Transport

50km Average distance from pallet supplier to build site in Europe. No long-haul freight.
0.8t CO₂ Transport emissions for one shell's materials. Compare to 8t for concrete-based construction.
Flatpack Everything ships flat. No wide loads. No escort vehicles. No crane required for assembly.
08 — Evidence

We're not asking for trust. We're publishing proof.

20+ sensors. 12 months. Every wall, joint, roof. Real-time public dashboard.

🌡️

Thermal Performance

Temperature and humidity sensors in every room, wall cavity, and roof space. Verifying U-value 0.22 W/m²K in practice, not just on paper.

📐

Structural Deflection

LVDT sensors on critical joints measuring load, settlement, and creep over 12 months. Data feeds back into CAD model improvements.

⚡

Energy Balance

Solar generation vs. consumption. Battery state. LED circuit loads. Proving off-grid viability with real winter data.

💧

Moisture & Breathability

Wood moisture content sensors in pallet cores. Verifying that the breathable wall assembly manages humidity correctly.

📊

Live Dashboard

All sensor data published in real time at data.hestiafoundation.org. Download raw CSV. Fork the analysis. Disagree with our conclusions — the data is yours.
