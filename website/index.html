The Ribbed Composite Panel (RCP)

Our building system uses interlocking EPAL pallets reinforced with I-joists and sheathed with OSB — engineered, tested, and monitored.

How It Works

Standard 1200×800 mm EPAL pallets are arranged in interlocking grids. I-joist reinforcement provides structural rigidity. OSB or cement-board sheathing creates a weathertight envelope. Mineral wool insulation ensures thermal comfort.

  • Wind loads per Eurocode 1
  • Seismic compliance per Eurocode 8
  • Thermal comfort 18–24°C without heating/cooling
  • 20–30 year lifespan with maintenance

Shell A Prototype — Q3 2026

Our first build will prove the system works, collect 12 months of sensor data, and produce a complete open-source build manual.

Build in Algarve, Portugal

A team of 4–6 people will construct the 37.5 m² Shell A over 10 active days in summer 2026. The prototype includes electrics, plumbing, insulation, and a 20-sensor monitoring network.

  • Target cost: €25,000 (materials + labour)
  • Independent structural testing by LNEC
  • University of Algarve student research partnership
  • Real-time sensor dashboard published openly
