# HESTIA TECHNICAL CALCULATIONS

## 1. SIP Load Capacity vs Hestia RCP

### Standard SIP (Styrofoam/EPS Core)
- OSB skins: 2 × 11mm
- EPS core: 100-200mm, density 15-20 kg/m³
- Shear strength of EPS: ~0.1-0.2 MPa (very low)
- OSB-to-EPS bond: adhesive only, no mechanical fastening
- **Governing failure**: OSB skin buckling or adhesive delamination under shear
- Typical SIP wall panel (1200×2400×150mm): ~8-12 kN/m² allowable load
- **Problem**: EPS provides almost no shear transfer. The panel acts as two independent thin plates.
- **Screw pull-out from EPS**: ~50-100 N per screw (useless for fixtures)

### Hestia RCP (Ribbed Composite Panel)
- OSB3 skins: 2 × 15mm (stronger than SIP's 11mm)
- Core: EPAL pallet timber, density ~450-550 kg/m³
- Timber shear strength: ~3-5 MPa (20-50× EPS)
- Mechanical fastening: ring-shank nails + PU adhesive = composite action
- **Governing failure**: OSB nail-head pull-through (easiest component to upgrade)
- **Shear transfer**: Timber stringers act as continuous shear connectors
- **Screw pull-out into timber**: ~800-1200 N per screw (10-20× EPS)
- **Result**: RCP is approximately 3-5× stronger in shear than equivalent-thickness SIP
- **Additional benefit**: Pallet blocks at corners create natural bearing points and fastening zones

## 2. Weight Calculation — 3 Pallets + 2× 15mm OSB Sheets

### 3 EPAL Euro Pallets
- Single EPAL pallet: ~20-25 kg (softwood, kiln-dried)
- **3 pallets: 75 kg**

### 2× OSB3 Sheets (2500 × 1250 × 15mm)
- OSB3 density: ~620 kg/m³
- Volume per sheet: 2.5 × 1.25 × 0.015 = 0.0469 m³
- Weight per sheet: 0.0469 × 620 = **29.1 kg**
- **2 sheets: 58.2 kg**

### Total Weight
- 3 pallets + 2 OSB sheets = **133.2 kg**
- This is the material for approximately 3 RCP panels (one OSB sheet covers ~3.25 pallet faces)
- **Per RCP panel (1200×800×145mm): ~44 kg**
- Compare: Equivalent SIP panel = ~25 kg (lighter but weaker)
- Compare: Equivalent timber frame wall (studs+insulation+plasterboard) = ~35-40 kg/m² → ~33 kg for 0.96m²

### Per-Panel Breakdown
| Component | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| EPAL pallet core | 25 kg |
| OSB3 skins (2× cut) | 17.8 kg |
| PU adhesive + nails | 1.5 kg |
| **TOTAL** | **~44 kg** |

## 3. Wood Material Volume

### Per RCP Panel
- Pallet timber volume: ~0.024 m³
- OSB volume (2 skins): 1.2 × 0.8 × 0.015 × 2 = 0.0288 m³
- **Total wood per panel: ~0.053 m³**

### For Complete 84m² Home (44 panels)
- Total timber: 44 × 0.053 = **2.33 m³**
- Plus I-joists (24 × 5m): ~0.6 m³
- Plus roof/floor decking: ~0.4 m³
- **Total structural wood: ~3.3 m³**
- Compare: Equivalent timber frame home: ~8-12 m³ of timber
- **Result: Hestia uses approximately 60-70% LESS wood than conventional timber frame**

## 4. Electricity Routing — Hollow Cavity Advantages

### Conventional Construction
- Solid studs at 400-600mm centres
- Must drill through studs (notching prohibited by code)
- Holes weaken structural members
- 20mm holes max in 100mm studs = tight fit
- Retrofit: impossible without demolition

### Hestia Pallet Cavity
- Pallet void space: ~60-65% of volume is empty
- Three stringers create vertical chases at 400mm centres (by design)
- Deck boards create horizontal chases every 120-150mm
- **Natural conduit network — no drilling required**
- Cables run freely between OSB faces
- Full 145mm depth available (vs 100mm stud cavity)
- Retrofit: remove one OSB face, re-route, re-fasten

### Routing Speed Comparison
| Task | Conventional | Hestia |
|------|-------------|--------|
| First-fix electrical | 2-3 days (drill, fish, seal) | 4-6 hours (lay in voids) |
| Cable capacity | 3-4 cables per hole | Unlimited in void |
| Retrofit addition | Demolition required | Remove face, add cable |
| Outlet placement | Fixed to stud positions | Anywhere on panel |

## 5. Blow-In Rock Wool Speed

### Cavity Volume (84m² home)
- Wall area: ~65m² (perimeter 26m × height 2.5m)
- Roof area: ~45m²
- Floor perimeter: ~15m²
- Total cavity: ~125m² × 0.145m = **18.1 m³**

### Installation Rate
- Professional blow-in contractor: 8-12 m³/hour
- **Time to fill entire home: 2-2.5 hours**
- Compare to batt installation: 2-3 days cutting, fitting, compressing
- Compare to spray foam: 1 day + 24hr curing + toxic off-gassing

### Material Required
- Rock wool density (blown): 35 kg/m³
- Total rock wool: 18.1 × 35 = **634 kg**
- Cost at €8-12/m²: ~€1,000-1,500 for complete home

## 6. Ground Screw Speed & Performance

### Specifications
- Diameter: 68mm helical
- Load capacity: 15-25 kN per screw (verified by manufacturer)
- Embedment depth: 1.2-2.5m depending on soil
- Material: Galvanized steel, 50-year design life

### Installation Rate
- Handheld hydraulic driver: 10-15 minutes per screw
- For 37.5m² module: 12-16 screws required
- **Total installation time: 3-4 hours for complete foundation**
- Crew: 2 people (one drives, one checks level)

### Comparison to Concrete Foundation
| Aspect | Concrete Slab | Ground Screws |
|--------|--------------|---------------|
| Excavation | 1-2 days + machinery | None |
| Formwork | 1 day | None |
| Pour | 4 hours + pump | N/A |
| Curing | 7-28 days | Immediate |
| Weather dependency | Cannot pour in rain | Any weather |
| Reversibility | Demolition only | Unscrew and reuse |
| Load capacity | 150-200 kN/m² | 15-25 kN per point |
| **Total time to weathertight** | **35-45 days** | **Same day** |

### Key Advantage
Ground screws transform foundation from a **month-long critical path** into a **half-day non-critical task**. The build starts immediately. No concrete truck. No curing anxiety. No weather dependency.

## 7. I-Joist Ubiquity in Construction

### Market Penetration
- I-joists used in **>80% of new timber frame construction** in Northern Europe
- Standard product at every timber merchant in EU/UK/US
- Manufacturers: Steico (DE), TJi (US), Boise Cascade (US), James Latham (UK)
- Available within 48 hours from stock

### Standard Sizes
| Depth | Span (domestic floor) | Span (roof) |
|-------|------------------------|-------------|
| 200mm | 4.5m | 5.5m |
| 240mm | 5.5m | 6.5m |
| 300mm | 6.5m | 8.0m |
| 360mm | 7.5m | 9.0m |

### Why I-Joists for Hestia
- Lighter than solid timber (same strength, 40% less weight)
- Straighter than solid timber (no warping, no knots)
- Longer spans without intermediate supports
- Cut and notch according to manufacturer tables
- **OSB web matches Hestia wall material** — same material philosophy
- Standard hanger brackets available everywhere
