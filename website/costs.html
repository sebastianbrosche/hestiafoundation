04 - Cost Transparency

Here is exactly where
the money goes.

Hard rules. No hidden costs. No markup. The materials cost for a complete 84 square metre family home in Portugal. Every line item. Every assumption. Published openly so you can verify, challenge, and improve it. A single parent on a median salary, with €15,000 saved and a €30,000 loan, can afford a three-bedroom home. It is our job to make that enough.

4.1 - Hard Rules

Non-negotiable cost caps

These are stated openly and held by design. If a build exceeds these numbers, the system has been assembled incorrectly, or the component specification has been violated.

1 The structural shell never costs more than €20,000 in materials for a 70-84m² home.
2 The complete materials cost never exceeds €50,000.
3 If labour is hired, it never exceeds €25,000. In a self-build, labour is free.

Why these numbers are achievable. The conventional construction industry charges what the market will bear. A €150,000 house contains perhaps €35,000 in materials. The remainder is labour markup, contractor overhead, architect fees, project management, financing costs, and profit margin at every layer. Hestia removes every layer between the materials and the occupant. The family buys the materials directly, follows the protocol, and builds. The only remaining costs are the materials themselves, a short engagement with a structural engineer for stamp approval, and whatever professional services the owner chooses to hire rather than do themselves.

4.2 - The Structural Shell

This is the weathertight structure: walls, roof, floor, foundation, windows, and doors. Everything that keeps wind and rain out. Nothing that makes it comfortable to live in yet. No concrete. No rebar. The house is over-engineered, but the cost is minimal.

TOTAL COMPLETE HOME~€50,000

The self-build labour advantage. In a conventional build, labour is 50-70% of total cost. In a Hestia self-build, labour is €0. The family, their friends, and their community do the work. The protocol is the skill transfer. One person who has built a Hestia home can teach ten people to build the next. The knowledge compounds.

If labour must be hired (elderly occupants, disability, time constraints), it is capped at €25,000. This is approximately 300 hours at €80/hour, roughly the time required for assembly and finishing by a small crew.

A single parent on a median salary, with €15,000 saved and a €30,000 loan, can afford a three-bedroom home. The total materials cost for a complete 84 square metre family home stays under €50,000. It is our job to make that enough. It is our job to make the numbers work. This is not charity. This is not subsidy. This is structural affordability, achieved by removing the intermediation that inflates cost, and by publishing the knowledge that enclosure has kept expensive.
