About Hestia

We're a non-profit in Lagos, Portugal, building an open-source construction system that anyone can use to build an affordable, durable home.

Our Mission

To design, document, and distribute open-source building systems that enable families to construct durable, affordable, energy-independent homes using locally available materials.

We believe housing is infrastructure, not a luxury. And we believe the knowledge of how to build should be a public good, not private property.

Core Values

🔓

Openness

All knowledge is shared. No patents, no secrets. Everything is published under CC BY-SA 4.0.

🏠

Accessibility

Housing is a human right, not a luxury. Our system costs €25,000 — not €150,000+.

♻️

Sustainability

Circular economy, low carbon, regenerative. We reuse waste pallets and avoid concrete.

👥

Community

Built by and for the people who live in it. Self-build workshops, not contractor dependency.

📊

Evidence

Decisions based on data, not ideology. 12-month sensor monitoring on every build.

Why "Hestia"?

In Greek mythology, Hestia is the goddess of the hearth, home, and domestic life. She was the first-born of the Olympians, and her role was to maintain the sacred fire — the center of every home. We named our foundation after her because we believe every family deserves a hearth: a safe, warm, permanent place to live.

Our Story

Hestia Foundation was founded in May 2026 in Lagos, Algarve, Portugal. The founder, Sebastian Brosche, identified a critical gap: while EU house prices have risen 60% since 2013 and Portugal's have risen 94%, no systematic, open-source, low-cost construction system existed for families to build their own homes.

The Ribbed Composite Panel (RCP) system — using standard EPAL Euro pallets as the primary structural element — emerged from research into circular construction, waste stream utilisation, and appropriate technology for the Global South. The innovation is not the pallet itself, but the engineering: interlocking grids, I-joist reinforcement, OSB sheathing, and mineral wool insulation, combined with a complete open-source build manual.
